## Executive Summary

The repository has grown to **~287 files across 17 standalone domains**, ~547,000 words. This represents a ~17% increase in file count and ~38% increase in word count since the May 16 report, driven primarily by domain tracker corrections catching files that had been added without tracker updates.

**Platform maturity**: The policy infrastructure is substantially complete. Most domains are at Phase 3 (Outcomes & Decision Rules) or beyond. The dominant remaining work is (1) Phase 4 execution sequencing within domains, (2) communication layer (public-facing overviews), and (3) a handful of genuine content gaps.

**Major developments since May 16:**

- **Foreign policy architecture is now coherent**: IDP + treaty lock-in + impeachment reform (enumerated triggers including unilateral treaty abandonment) + electoral reform (breaks the two-party volatility cycle) = a credible allied commitment architecture for the first time. UN electoral advisory letter creates a distinctive international legitimacy signal. The "structural durability problem" for allies is addressed at three levels simultaneously: treaty mechanism, executive accountability, and underlying political system reform.

- **Presidential powers sunset statute** logged as cross-domain gap (NS + Trade + DI): single statute with two provisions — retroactive sunset of open-ended congressional authority grants (AUMFs 2001/2002, IEEPA tariff invocations, Section 232, TPA) on a scheduled basis unless reauthorized; mandatory prospective sunset on all future congressional delegations of executive authority. Democratic_Integrity is the canonical home; NS and Trade cross-reference. This pairs with the treaty architecture: treaties as hard-to-enter/hard-to-exit durability instruments; delegated emergency powers as time-bounded instruments requiring democratic reauthorization — currently inverted.

- **Social domain**: Culture_War_Issues subdomain added (6 files, ~18,750 words) — Democratic Settlement Framework approach to abortion, trans issues, guns, conversion therapy, immigration (draft), and overview. Trans issues at v10. The framing is consistent with the platform's anti-binary architecture: start from the underlying harm, not the rhetorical battle. Two files (abortion, conversion therapy) had missing YAML — fixed.

- **Institutional-Modernization**: Government OS Upgrade brief written (v1.0) — closes the Process_Redesign TODO. Articulates how execution instruments (PCA, DoDA, Execution Corps, SSI/UPI, WCA, ISA, RMS) collectively constitute a civilizational software upgrade with update/rollback/versioning logic.

- **Codetermination explicitly scoped for AI transition**: The AI workforce transition scope — 90-day notice requirement, workforce impact assessment — is now in the brief. Codetermination is the near-term stabilization mechanism against mass white-collar automation shock. The globalization parallel: deindustrialization took 40 years and caused political destabilization we're still living with; AI automation could hit a wider workforce faster. Codetermination doesn't ban automation — it introduces a negotiation layer that forces pacing and burden-sharing, same mechanism that worked in Germany.

- **Pharma recoupment + IDP pharmaceutical chapter**: The recoupment model is primarily a US fix (the US pays 3-4x European prices with no cost-grounding mechanism) but the attestation data it generates would transform allied negotiating positions. European HTA systems do value-based assessment; they lack cost-side data. Recoupment attestation shared through IDP = coordinated allied negotiation from verified cost baseline. Design principle: negotiate higher transparent return parameters rather than opaque carveouts — keeps architecture intact, removes capture vulnerability, preserves incentive to document real R&D investment.

**Platform framing note**: The platform's political identity is best understood as: fiscally serious (what the fiscal conservative label was supposed to mean — structural cost management without cutting entitlements); settlement-oriented on social issues (refuses the mobilization function of culture war, starts from harm reduction); strongly pro-worker, pro-housing, pro-energy, pro-America; and explicitly hostile to people who profit from broken systems. The "Americans need to stop getting robbed" framing is the unifying thread across pharma pricing, financial rent extraction, campaign finance capture, housing cost, and elite legal impunity.

## Cross-Cutting Infrastructure Status

### SSI / UPI — Platform Infrastructure Layer
SSI and UPI are the most cross-referenced instruments in the platform. Referenced in Healthcare (pharma attestation, patient privacy), Budget (dynasty tax, pre-seed, IRS attestation), Manufacturing (workforce attestation), National_Security (IDP digital layer), Democratic_Integrity (voter registration), Immigration (visa tracking), Labor (portable benefits). They are infrastructure, not policy.

**Current state**: Architectural briefs (~920/776 words) establish correct design principles (ZKP consent, provider blindness, privacy-preserving routing). Full technical implementation specs are the domain's most urgent Phase 4 need.

**Pre-deployment strategy**: Third-party wallet providers building SSI infrastructure pre-administration compresses Year 2-4 timeline to Day 1. Government role: credential issuance into existing wallets (IRS, SSA, DMV, state licensing boards). Private wallet competition is the primary inoculation against "mark of the beast" centralization attack. Not yet documented as formal strategy.
