# Regulatory Modernization Corps (RMC)

## Executive Summary

The Regulatory Modernization Corps (RMC) is a **standing execution body** tasked with modernizing the procedural architecture of American governance without altering substantive law.

Its mandate is structural, not ideological.

The RMC:
- Maps and maintains the national regulatory topology.
- Identifies redundancy, conflict, and latency.
- Designs and administers Regulatory Modernization Sandboxes (RMS).
- Issues bounded procedural waivers within sandbox environments.
- Forks regulatory pathways under defined authority.
- Publishes empirical performance results.
- Recommends legislative harmonization packages to Congress.

The RMC does not:
- Repeal statute.
- Alter substantive standards.
- Replace representative governance.
- Exercise policy-making authority.

It operates as a refactoring engine for regulatory process.

---

# I. Institutional Status

The RMC is established as a **Standing Execution Corps** under a permanent but narrowly defined statutory charter.

The standing body:
- Maintains institutional memory.
- Operates cross-sector.
- Publishes public regulatory artifacts.

Each sandbox deployment is executed through a **Mission-Bound Execution Corps instantiation** with automatic sunset.

The RMC owns *process architecture*, not policy outcomes.

---

# II. Scope of Authority

## A. Procedural Authority

Within statutory bounds, the RMC may:

- Consolidate duplicative procedural reviews.
- Parallelize sequential agency processes.
- Establish defined decision clocks where authorized.
- Designate lead coordinating authorities.
- Create unified administrative records.
- Standardize interagency data exchange.
- Certify Alternative Procedural Compliance Pathways.
- Issue time-bound procedural waivers within RMS environments.
- Fork procedural pathways for pilot jurisdictions.

All modifications must preserve substantive statutory standards.

## B. Waiver Authority (Sandbox-Only)

Within a Regulatory Modernization Sandbox, the RMC may issue:

- Temporary procedural waivers.
- Sequencing substitutions.
- Review consolidations.
- Equivalent compliance pathway approvals.

Waivers:
- Are time-limited.
- Are geographically or sectorally bounded.
- Cannot alter substantive law.
- Cannot eliminate judicial review.
- Automatically sunset unless renewed.

This authority is confined to sandbox designation.

---

# III. Regulatory Modernization Sandboxes (RMS)

RMS are controlled procedural experimentation environments.

Each RMS must include:

- Clear geographic or sectoral boundaries.
- Defined baseline metrics.
- Pre-registered performance indicators.
- Defined acceptable performance bands.
- Automatic performance triggers.
- Explicit rollback protocols.
- Public reporting requirements.
- Automatic sunset clause.

Participation is voluntary for states and municipalities.

Federal projects may qualify independently under statutory authorization.

---

# IV. Regulatory Version Control & Forking

The RMC operates a regulatory version-control architecture integrated with the Unified Law and Regulation Repository (ULRR).

Each procedural pathway modified under RMS receives:

- Version designation (e.g., v1.0, v1.1, v2.0).
- Structured diff logs.
- Machine-readable change records.
- Plain-English “What Changed” summaries.
- Public subscription feeds.

States and jurisdictions may fork procedural pathways within RMS authority.

Example:

Housing Review v2.0 (RMS Fork)
- Parallel intake
- Consolidated environmental hearing
- Defined 12-month shot clock

Dashboards compare forked pathways against baseline jurisdictions.

All forks are transparent, diffable, and reversible.

---

# V. Automatic Performance Review Protocol

Each RMS must encode predefined triggers across three domains:

1. Objective Performance Metrics  
2. Externality Thresholds  
3. Litigation Escalation Signals  

If trigger thresholds are breached:

- Automatic 90-day review begins.
- Findings are published.
- Parameter adjustments are proposed.
- Version increment or rollback decision issued.
- Public explanation is mandatory.

The RMC shall:

- Never reflexively expand RMS scope based solely on positive results.
- Never reflexively suspend RMS based on isolated negative events.

All action must flow through trigger logic.

Rollback is treated as institutional competence.

---

# VI. Co-Deployment Model

RMC personnel may co-deploy alongside:

- Public Capital Authority (PCA) teams.
- Federal regulatory agencies.
- State permitting authorities.
- Sector-specific Execution Corps units.

Co-deployment includes:

- Delegated procedural authority.
- Fixed decision clocks.
- Multi-signature approval thresholds.
- Real-time friction removal.

RMC operates at the point of administrative friction, not through advisory memoranda.

---

# VII. Diagnostic & Legislative Interface

For each RMS, the RMC must submit to Congress:

1. Procedural Diff Summary  
2. Outcome Delta Analysis  
3. Environmental / Safety Impact Assessment  
4. Litigation Profile  
5. Causal Inference Appendix  
6. Counterfactual Modeling  

Reports must be:

- Machine-readable.
- Plain-language accessible.
- Publicly archived.

The RMC may recommend statutory harmonization but cannot enact it.

---

# VIII. Governance Structure

The RMC Board consists of:

- Execution Lead
- Legal Envelope & Compliance Lead
- Data & Evaluation Lead
- Federalism & Intergovernmental Lead
- Rotating Sector Experts (fixed, non-renewable terms)

Safeguards include:

- Term limits.
- Cooling-off periods.
- Mandatory public logging of escalations.
- Prohibition on scope expansion absent statutory authority.
- Periodic congressional review.

No single officer may unilaterally authorize RMS designation.

---

# IX. Transparency & Public Repository

All regulatory pathway versions, diff logs, and performance dashboards are publicly accessible.

Features include:

- Open regulatory repositories.
- Version comparison tools.
- AI-generated summaries of changes.
- Subscription-based update notifications.
- Public audit trails.
- Historical rollback records.

The RMC treats regulatory architecture as auditable infrastructure.

---

# X. Relationship to Capital Allocation

The RMC does not allocate capital.

It publishes procedural friction metrics.

The Public Capital Authority may incorporate these metrics into risk modeling and portfolio decisions.

Institutional independence is preserved.

---

# XI. Explicit Non-Goals

The RMC is not:

- A deregulatory override mechanism.
- A centralized policy-making body.
- A substitute for Congress.
- A permanent emergency authority.
- A political advocacy institution.

Its mandate is procedural modernization.

---

# Guiding Principle

Democratic institutions determine objectives.  
Substantive law defines standards.  
The Regulatory Modernization Corps refactors process.  
Results are measured, published, and reversible.  
Legitimacy derives from transparency and constraint.
