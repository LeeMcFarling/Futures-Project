# Department of Data and Accountability (DoDA)

## Purpose and Rationale

Modern democratic governance suffers from a persistent structural failure: the inability to reliably determine whether public interventions are working.

Policy outcomes are measured unevenly across agencies with conflicting incentives. Success criteria are often vague, revised midstream, or defined only after large fiscal and political commitments have been made. Programs persist despite weak performance, while successful interventions scale slowly due to uncertainty or contested interpretation of results.

This environment produces ideological conflict in place of empirical evaluation, weakens institutional credibility, and contributes to the accumulation of permanent structures whose effectiveness is rarely verified.

The Department of Data and Accountability exists to correct this failure by establishing a trusted national capability for outcome certification and trigger maintenance.

---

## Institutional Position

DoDA is a **standing institutional backbone established by Congress** and operates alongside execution instruments such as the Execution Corps and capital deployment bodies such as the Public Capital Authority.

Unlike execution institutions, DoDA holds no delivery authority.  
Unlike capital institutions, it allocates no financial resources.  
Unlike regulatory agencies, it enforces no compliance regimes.

Its authority is limited to measurement, certification, and publication.

Execution institutions act on certified outcomes.  
Legislatures define objectives and decision pathways.  
Markets and jurisdictions retain operational responsibility.

This separation ensures that programs are not evaluated by the same institutions responsible for their success or failure.

---

## Mandate

The mandate of DoDA is to ensure that federally authorized interventions are instrumented, measured, and concluded according to predefined outcome logic.

This includes:

- validating methodological integrity of pilot designs  
- verifying baseline construction and comparison frameworks  
- certifying whether predefined success or termination thresholds have been met  
- publishing standardized performance dashboards  
- maintaining the integrity of policy trigger systems over time  

DoDA does not define what success means.  
It determines whether success conditions have been achieved.

---

## Trigger Integrity Doctrine

All federally authorized pilots and phased programs are expected to include predefined performance instrumentation.

Authorizing instruments must specify intended outcome direction, baseline construction approach, evaluation windows, expansion triggers, and termination criteria. Proposals lacking measurable outcome logic may be returned for revision prior to execution authorization.

DoDA’s role is limited to:

- methodological validation  
- baseline verification  
- statistical certification  
- public publication  

Parameter revision authority may be delegated only within predefined adjustment bands established at authorization.

This doctrine enables democratic institutions to pre-commit to adaptive decision pathways rather than relying on discretionary interpretation after outcomes emerge.

---

## National Policy Telemetry

DoDA operates the national evidence observability layer. It defines and maintains
the APIs, schemas, study identifiers, audit logs, routing rules, and reporting
standards that make research and public-program evaluation legible across
institutions. DoDA does not replace domain authorities. It makes their outputs
comparable, traceable, and auditable — the data spine connecting measurement
across the system without centralizing scientific judgment.

DoDA specifies what observability infrastructure needs to do. The Institutional
Modernization Corps builds that infrastructure to DoDA-certified specifications.
Agencies and certified providers operate the systems once transferred. DoDA
certifies that operating systems continue to meet published standards over time.
DoDA does not build or operate the systems itself.

DoDA does not operate identity wallets (SSI rails), payment systems (UPI mechanisms), or sectoral digital infrastructure.

These systems are operated by certified public, private, or hybrid entities under standardized interoperability and reporting requirements.

Participation in certified telemetry reporting is mandatory for programs, jurisdictions, or providers receiving federal capital support, execution deployment, or regulatory backstop.

DoDA defines reporting standards, certifies compliant data interfaces, aggregates anonymized performance datasets, and publishes comparative dashboards.

This structure preserves infrastructure innovation while ensuring comparability of policy outcomes.

---

## Methodological Stewardship

DoDA maintains national reference datasets and evaluation protocols that enable consistent comparison of interventions across jurisdictions and time periods.

These include:

- causal inference frameworks for pilot evaluation  
- standardized performance metrics where appropriate  
- recalibration schedules for trigger thresholds  
- publication of methodological updates  

Methodological continuity is treated as a public good.  
Frequent redesign of metrics for political convenience is treated as institutional failure.

---

## Oversight, Transparency, and Legitimacy

DoDA operates under continuous congressional oversight focused on methodological integrity rather than operational outcomes.

It publishes:

- certification notices  
- comparative outcome dashboards  
- evaluation protocols  
- recalibration reports  
- post-pilot performance summaries  

Performance data is presented descriptively and symmetrically.

Trust in DoDA derives from demonstrated neutrality and accuracy over multiple policy cycles.

---

## Scope Boundary

DoDA is prohibited from:

- originating policy objectives  
- directing execution sequencing  
- allocating capital  
- operating citizen-facing infrastructure  
- engaging in political advocacy  

It measures reality.  
It does not prescribe ideology.

---

## Institutional Durability

Unlike mission-bound execution instruments, DoDA is a standing institution due to the need for continuity in measurement frameworks and reference datasets.

Its mandate is subject to periodic statutory review.  
Its operational authority is bounded.  
Its methodological independence is structurally protected through staggered leadership terms and transparent recalibration procedures.

This design preserves long-term signal stability while preventing institutional drift.

---

## End State

When fully deployed, DoDA completes the structural separation between policy authorization, policy execution, and policy evaluation.

- Legislatures define objectives and decision rules.  
- Execution instruments deliver bounded interventions.  
- Capital institutions deploy risk deliberately.  
- DoDA certifies outcomes and maintains trigger integrity.

Democratic governance becomes capable of learning visibly, scaling success deliberately, and terminating failure without crisis.

Policy becomes testable.  
State capacity becomes adaptive.  
Institutional trust becomes renewable.
