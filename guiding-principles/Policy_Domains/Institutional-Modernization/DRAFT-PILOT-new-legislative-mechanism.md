# Sample Futures Legislative Instrument
## Regional Wildfire Risk Reduction Pilot Act

---

### Section 1. Objective

The purpose of this Act is to reduce catastrophic wildfire acreage and associated economic loss in designated high-risk regions through deployment of targeted mitigation interventions, coordinated execution capacity, and adaptive scaling based on certified performance outcomes.

Congress finds that current wildfire mitigation efforts are fragmented across jurisdictions, inconsistently funded, and weakly evaluated for effectiveness.

---

### Section 2. Authorized Intervention Envelope

Congress authorizes a five-year pilot intervention in eligible wildfire risk zones.

The intervention may include:

- fuel-load reduction programs  
- resilient infrastructure deployment  
- defensible space initiatives  
- early detection technology integration  
- community evacuation readiness improvements  

Execution shall be conducted through designated Execution Corps formations operating within this statutory envelope.

Operational sequencing and implementation methodology are delegated to execution authorities.

---

### Section 3. Capital Exposure and Financing

Congress authorizes a capital exposure envelope not to exceed $18 billion over the pilot duration.

The Public Capital Authority may deploy standardized capital instruments including milestone-based grants with clawback provisions, repayable resilience credits, and first-loss financing structures to support mitigation investments.

Capital deployment shall prioritize crowd-in of private and state funding where feasible.

Portfolio-level performance reporting shall be published quarterly.

---

### Section 4. Performance Instrumentation and Triggers

The intervention shall be instrumented with predefined performance thresholds.

Primary outcome metric:  
Statistically significant reduction in catastrophic wildfire acreage relative to matched control regions.

Secondary metrics include:

- reduction in suppression cost per acre  
- reduction in insured and uninsured loss exposure  
- improvement in evacuation execution time  
- reduction in post-fire infrastructure disruption  

Expansion Authority:  
If certified outcome thresholds are met within three years, Congress may authorize geographic expansion and capital envelope adjustment.

Termination Authority:  
If no statistically significant improvement is certified within four years, new capital deployment shall pause pending congressional review.

---

### Section 5. Outcome Certification

The Department of Data and Accountability shall:

- validate baseline construction methodology  
- certify statistical significance of outcome measures  
- publish comparative performance dashboards  
- issue interim and final certification notices  

Certification does not determine policy continuation.  
It determines whether authorized decision thresholds have been reached.

---

### Section 6. Execution Oversight

Congressional oversight shall focus on:

- integrity of trigger design  
- adherence to authorized intervention envelope  
- capital exposure discipline  
- credibility of certification processes  

Operational micromanagement of execution activities is prohibited absent statutory amendment.

---

### Section 7. Sunset and Adaptive Review

The pilot intervention shall sunset automatically after five years unless reauthorized.

Congress shall conduct adaptive review hearings following certification milestones to determine:

- scaling  
- redesign  
- termination  
- or transition to standing resilience frameworks.
