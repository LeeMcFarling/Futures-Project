## Overview

Care delivery is reorganized around how care is actually used. Services that are predictable and shoppable operate as markets. Services that are time-critical or non-shoppable operate as utilities or regulated risk pools. The objective is rapid price reduction for routine care, preserved access for emergencies, and simplified incentives for providers and patients.

The system is divided into four layers:

1. **Primary Care** — longitudinal, relationship-based  
2. **Urgent Care** — episodic, time-sensitive but stable  
3. **Scheduled Surgery & Complex Episodes** — low-frequency, high-cost  
4. **Emergency & Trauma Care** — non-shoppable, life-critical  

---

## Primary Care — Direct-to-Consumer Market

Primary care functions as the default front door to the system, and includes imaging and laboratory services. 

### Payment Models
- Subscription-based care where continuity adds value (prevention, chronic disease, coordination)
- Per-visit pricing where continuity does not add value
- Insurance billing and prior authorization are prohibited

### Pricing Rules
- Prices must be posted, binding, and machine-readable
- Subscription scope must be explicit (access standards, panel size, included services)
- No membership required to access episodic services

### Subsidies
- Government support is person-based, not provider-based
- Capped, income-linked credits applied at enrollment or point of sale 
- Overtime, employers may co-subsidize memberships without creating lock-in

---

## Urgent Care — Episodic Market

Urgent care handles time-sensitive but stable conditions and prevents inappropriate ER use.

### Payment Model
- Pay-per-episode only
- Subscriptions prohibited

### Pricing Rules
- Standardized, all-in bundles for common presentations (imaging, lacerations, fractures, infections)
- Posted prices visible before treatment whenever feasible

### Triage Boundary for Primary and Urgent Care
- Mandatory escalation to EMS/ER when standardized emergency thresholds are met
- Legal safe harbor for rapid escalation
- Prohibition on billing urgent care services as emergency care

---

## Scheduled Surgery & Complex Episodes — Catastrophic Coverage + Regulated Competition

Scheduled surgeries and complex procedures are covered through catastrophic insurance and delivered through hospitals and regional surgery centers.

### Payment Model
- All-in bundled pricing (facility, clinicians, anesthesia, devices, follow-up)
- Catastrophic insurance pays standardized bundles
- Reference pricing caps public contribution

### Provider Competition
- Surgery centers and hospitals compete on price and outcomes
- No facility-fee games or unbundled add-ons

### Eligibility & Safety
- Risk stratification determines ASC vs hospital eligibility
- Mandatory transfer protocols for complications

---

## Emergency & Trauma Care — Public Utility

Emergency and trauma care are treated as essential civic infrastructure.

### Access & Readiness
- Public guarantee is availability and readiness, not open-ended payment
- Communities retain emergency access regardless of provider financial distress

### Funding Model
- Regional global budgets tied to staffing, capacity, surge readiness, and audited outcomes
- No per-item or per-incident billing incentives

### Price Discipline
- Budget-setting and oversight discipline costs upstream
- Emergency designation does not entitle providers to unlimited reimbursement

---

## Cross-Cutting Standards

### National Service Ontology
- Single standardized codebook for services, bundles, and episodes

### Transparency
- Mandatory price publication across all care layers
- Public comparison interface using a common data model

### Data & Accountability
- Timestamped triage logs and outcome reporting
- Independent audits and anti-gaming enforcement

---

## Transition & Pilots
- Phased rollout beginning with volunteer pilot states
- Standing execution authority manages stabilization and rapid response
- Weekly public operational briefs document progress, failures, and corrections

---

## Bottom Line

Primary and urgent care become cheap and legible. Surgery becomes shoppable and insurable. Emergency care becomes reliable infrastructure. Care delivery stops being a billing maze and starts behaving like a system designed on purpose.
