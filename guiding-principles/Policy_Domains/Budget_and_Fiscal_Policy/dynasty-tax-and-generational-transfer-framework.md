## Purpose

The American capital gains system has a structural gap at the generational
transfer. When an individual dies holding appreciated assets, the embedded
gain — the difference between the original cost basis and the current
value — is permanently eliminated. Heirs inherit the position with a
stepped-up basis equal to its value at the date of death. The accumulated
tax liability disappears entirely. A position that compounded for fifty
years, generating hundreds of millions or billions in unrealized gain,
passes to the next generation as though it were purchased at its current
price.

This is not a minor technical feature of the estate system. It is the
mechanism through which permanent dynastic insulation is achieved at
extreme scale. An individual who holds appreciated assets indefinitely
and never sells — never triggering realization through sale — faces
no tax on that appreciation during their lifetime if they borrow rather
than sell, and no tax at death because the step-up eliminates the gain
on transfer. The borrowing-triggered partial realization framework closes
the borrowing path. This framework closes the generational transfer path.
Together they make the permanent, frictionless elimination of embedded
gain structurally unavailable.

This brief is the third instrument in the anti-aristocracy framework.
The borrowing trigger closes active monetization. This framework closes
the generational transfer and the holding-forever path simultaneously:
if you hold indefinitely and never borrow, you eventually die, and under
this framework that transfer is a realization event rather than a
basis-reset opportunity. The holding-forever strategy has no permanent
exit from the tax system for positions above the threshold.

## The Threshold and Its Logic

The $50 million indexed threshold that governs this framework is the
same threshold used across the anti-aristocracy architecture and the
universal superannuation system. It is symmetrical in intent: below it,
the country is actively invested in your wealth accumulation — seed
capital, a protected pass-through architecture for compounding within
purpose-governed wrappers, and clean intergenerational transmission
within the four-bucket structure. Above it, the social contract changes.
The same number that defines the ceiling of the national gift defines
the floor of the dynasty obligation.

That symmetry is not rhetorical. It reflects a genuine distinction in
what the system is designed to do. Below $50 million, the full upside
of wealth creation remains intact. Ordinary successful entrepreneurs,
professionals with significant accumulated savings, family businesses
with substantial assets, and upper-middle-class households with real
estate and investment portfolios are entirely outside this framework.
The full incentive to build, invest, and accumulate operates without
structural friction. Wealth transfers within this range to heirs
tax-free under the superannuation system's protected regime.

Above $50 million, the position has crossed into a different category
— one where the scale of accumulated appreciation, and the capacity
to deploy sophisticated planning to prevent that appreciation from
ever interacting with the tax system, is qualitatively different from
anything available to ordinary wealth. The dynasty tax does not cap
accumulation at $50 million. It does not prevent wealth above that
threshold from being held, managed, or transferred. It ensures that
the embedded gain — the appreciation that has compounded inside that
position, often for decades, often with meaningful public contribution
through infrastructure, markets, legal systems, and consumer purchasing
power — is recognized at the generational transfer rather than
permanently eliminated by it.

## Triggering Events

Unrealized gains on total assets above the $50 million indexed threshold
are recognized as realized and taxed at the applicable capital gains
rate upon the first of two triggering events.

The primary trigger is death. At the death of the asset holder, all
assets above the threshold are treated as sold at fair market value.
Embedded gain — the difference between the current fair market value
and the original cost basis — is recognized as a realization event
for the portion of total assets above the threshold. The step-up basis
that currently eliminates this gain at death does not apply above the
threshold. The heir inherits the position at the original cost basis,
and the capital gains tax liability is settled from the estate through
the wind-down process described below.

The secondary trigger is the 100-year holding mark. For assets held
in trust, family limited partnerships, or any other structure designed
to defer realization beyond a natural lifetime — including perpetual
trust architectures that are specifically designed to avoid a death
event — a mandatory realization event occurs at the 100-year mark
from the date of original contribution or formation. The 100-year
trigger closes the dynastic trust loophole. Assets cannot be
structured to permanently defer realization by routing around the
death trigger through perpetual vehicle design.

In practice, the 100-year trigger will rarely fire. Its function is
to close the theoretical permanent holding path — the structure that
never dies, never sells, and never borrows, compounding indefinitely
behind a trust that exists specifically to prevent any recognition
event. That path is closed by the trigger's existence, which is
sufficient. The death trigger closes the common case. The 100-year
trigger closes the engineering case.

## What Is Subject to Realization

At a triggering event, all assets are aggregated across all legal
forms of ownership attributable to the holder or estate: superannuation
accounts, real estate, private and public market holdings, trust
interests, partnership interests, closely held business equity, and
all other capital. The $50 million indexed threshold is applied against
the aggregate. Only the portion above the threshold is subject to
realization.

A holder with $80 million in total assets faces realization on the
$30 million above the threshold, not on the full $80 million. The
threshold is a floor, not a cliff. The embedded gain attributable to
the above-threshold portion is calculated proportionally across the
portfolio using the same unified portfolio treatment established in
the borrowing-triggered realization framework.

Assets held in trust, through family limited partnerships, or through
other structures are attributed to the beneficial holder for purposes
of the threshold calculation. Economic ownership, not legal form,
determines attribution. This is consistent with the economic substance
doctrine that governs the borrowing trigger and the corporate
reciprocity framework. The test is the same throughout the architecture:
legal form is irrelevant, economic reality is determinative.

## The Wind-Down — Not a Firesale

The operational detail for illiquid, closely held, operating, and
hard-to-value assets — including valuation methodology, the three
settlement lanes, interest and accretion rules, recurring checkpoints,
the PCA administration vs. custody distinction, and anti-abuse
architecture — is established in the companion brief: the Dynasty
Illiquid Asset Realization Framework. This section states the governing
principle. The companion brief defines how it works.

The most important design element in this framework — the one that
determines whether it is administratively workable and politically
defensible — is the wind-down architecture. Assets subject to
realization above the threshold are not liquidated immediately. A
forced sale of a large concentrated position, compressed into the
estate settlement period, would be disruptive to markets, destructive
to the value of the estate, and genuinely harmful to shareholders,
employees, and counterparties who had no role in the triggering event.
That is not the design. The design is orderly, planned, and calibrated
to protect all parties.

At the triggering event, the recognized obligation is recorded through the Public Capital Authority or a designated PCA-administered estate settlement facility. By default, the family retains ownership and operational control of the underlying assets subject to a recorded security interest, statutory drawdown schedule, reporting obligations, and enforcement remedies. Asset transfer to PCA occurs only through prepayment election, default, or structured settlement. Tax liability is recognized at the triggering event — the gain is deemed realized at that moment at fair market value. But payment of that liability, and any disposition of the underlying assets, occurs through a statutory wind-down schedule calibrated by asset class.

Publicly traded positions are wound down over a defined schedule
sufficient to allow orderly market absorption without price distortion.
The specific schedule is set in statute with asset-class parameters
and is not subject to discretionary extension. Private equity and
closely held business interests — where an immediate sale would
destroy going-concern value and harm employees and partners — are
wound down over a longer schedule with independent valuation and
structured disposition. Real estate is handled through structured
disposition rather than forced sale, with timelines that allow orderly
marketing rather than distressed auction. The wind-down schedule is
the same for all estates subject to the framework. It is not
negotiable, not subject to political accommodation, and not extended
through administrative delay.

The PCA's role in administering the wind-down is not incidental. The
PCA is already the institution responsible for deploying public capital
deliberately and recycling it as markets mature. The capital that flows
through the dynasty tax wind-down — the embedded gain recognized at
the generational transfer — becomes explicit, accountable public capital
rather than disappearing into the general fund. It is deployed through
PCA instruments toward congressionally defined public objectives,
recycled when those objectives are achieved, and subject to the same
performance measurement and sunset discipline that governs every other
PCA instrument. The dynasty tax is not confiscation. It is the
recognition of a gain that has been deferred, collected in an orderly
way, and deployed toward the public system that helped generate it.

## The Prepayment Election

Holders who anticipate crossing the threshold — or who have already
crossed it and prefer to settle their liability on their own terms
rather than leaving it to estate administration — may elect to prepay
capital gains liability on a voluntary schedule prior to a triggering
event.

Prepayment is irrevocable once elected for the designated assets.
It locks in the capital gains rate at the time of election, eliminating
rate uncertainty for holders who expect rates to rise or who simply
prefer certainty. It transfers the designated assets to PCA
administration on the prepayment schedule, moving them out of the
estate and into orderly disposition on terms the holder controls.
It eliminates the realization event for those assets at death —
prepaid assets do not go through the estate wind-down because the
liability has already been settled.

The prepayment election is not a discount mechanism. The rate is the
same as it would be at the triggering event. It is a timing and
certainty mechanism. It benefits holders who prefer to control the
process — who would rather settle at a known rate on a known schedule
than leave the question to estate administration under whatever
conditions prevail at death. It benefits the public by delivering
capital to the PCA in an orderly, planned manner rather than through
a compressed estate process. And it reduces administrative complexity
at death, which benefits the estate, the heirs, and the settlement
process.

For holders with large illiquid positions — private company founders,
major real estate owners, controlling shareholders in closely held
businesses — the prepayment election provides a planning tool that
does not require selling the underlying position during their lifetime.
They can designate assets for prepayment, agree to the wind-down
schedule, lock in the rate, and know that the matter is settled. That
certainty has genuine value and the framework is designed to make the
election attractive rather than to punish those who use it.

## Anti-Avoidance

Borrowing against above-threshold assets does not defer, reduce, or
eliminate capital gains liability under this framework. This provision
is redundant with the borrowing-triggered partial realization framework
for borrowing that occurs during the holder's lifetime — the borrowing
trigger already closes that path. The anti-avoidance provision here
addresses the interaction between the two frameworks at the moment
of death: a holder who has borrowed extensively against above-threshold
assets, reducing the equity position's apparent value, cannot use that
leverage to reduce the fair market value recognized at death. Realization
occurs at fair market value of the underlying asset, not at net equity
after debt.

Transfers to related parties within ten years of a triggering event
are clawed back into the estate for threshold calculation purposes.
A holder cannot reduce their above-threshold position below the
framework's reach by gifting assets to family members in the years
preceding death. The ten-year lookback is consistent with existing
gift tax clawback provisions and extends them explicitly to this
framework's threshold calculation.

Transfers to charitable vehicles are not automatically exempt from
the threshold calculation. A charitable transfer that functions as
a dynasty preservation tool — one that provides ongoing economic
benefit to the holder's family through investment returns, employment,
or distribution rights — is attributed to the holder for threshold
purposes under the economic substance doctrine. Genuine charitable
transfers that irrevocably remove the holder's family from economic
benefit are exempt. The test is substance, not form.

## Interaction with the Borrowing Trigger

This framework and the borrowing-triggered partial realization framework
are designed as complementary instruments that close different structural
paths without creating double taxation on the same gain.

The borrowing trigger operates during the holder's lifetime, recognizing
a proportional fraction of embedded gain when appreciated assets are
monetized through borrowing or equivalent structures. Each triggering
event under the borrowing framework adjusts the cost basis of the
relevant portion of the portfolio upward — the gain recognized is the
gain realized, and that portion of the position's basis is stepped up
accordingly.

At death, the dynasty tax framework applies to the remaining embedded
gain — the gain that was never recognized during the holder's lifetime
through sale, borrowing, or other realization event. The two frameworks
operate on different portions of the same position's gain without
overlapping. A holder who borrowed extensively during their lifetime,
triggering proportional realization events that reduced the embedded
unrealized gain, faces a smaller dynasty tax obligation at death
because the basis has been stepped up through those lifetime events.
A holder who never borrowed and never sold faces the full embedded
gain at death. The system is internally consistent: every dollar of
gain is recognized exactly once, either during the holder's lifetime
when it is accessed or at the generational transfer when it is
transferred.

## Why This Is Not Confiscation

The dynasty tax is a capital gains tax on appreciation that has never
been recognized — gain that accrued over years or decades, often with
meaningful contribution from public infrastructure, markets, legal
systems, and consumer purchasing power, and that has been deferred
through the holding-forever strategy until the moment of generational
transfer.

It is not a tax on the asset itself. It is not a tax on wealth merely
for existing. It is not a forced sale of a going-concern business or
a family farm. The wind-down schedule ensures that no asset must be
sold immediately or under distress. The prepayment election ensures
that holders who want certainty can obtain it on their own terms. The
$50 million threshold ensures that the overwhelming majority of
Americans — including most successful entrepreneurs, family business
owners, and accumulated savers — are entirely outside the framework.

What the dynasty tax closes is a specific mechanism: the permanent
elimination of embedded gain at death through basis step-up, available
only to positions large enough to have been held through a lifetime
without any realization. That mechanism is not a design feature of a
functioning capital gains system. It is a structural consequence of
applying a realization-based system to positions that never need to
realize because their scale makes indefinite deferral economically
dominant. This framework corrects that consequence by treating the
generational transfer as the realization event it economically is.

## Super-Account Contributions and Anti-Fragmentation

The ten-year lookback for related-party transfers extends explicitly to
privileged super-account contributions. A holder cannot reduce their
above-threshold position by fragmenting assets across children's and
descendants' super accounts in the years preceding a triggering event
and then claim that no dynasty threshold was crossed.

For dynasty-threshold purposes, large transfers into children's or
descendants' super accounts remain attributable to the transferor or
family-control group when they come from related parties, trusts,
estates, controlled entities, or entities with retained donor influence.
The ten-year lookback covers super-account contributions, Bucket B and
Bucket C transfers, trust substitutions, and controlled-entity deposits.

You can capitalize your children. You cannot use their accounts as
fragments of a hidden dynasty balance sheet.

SSI attestation is the enforcement mechanism. To make a privileged
transfer into the super-account wrapper, a trust, family LLC, estate,
foundation, holding company, or family office must provide an SSI-backed
attestation of its beneficial owners, control persons, authorized
signers, and relationship to the beneficiary. The system does not need
to guess whether entities are related; it requires relationship
attestation as the price of entering the privileged wrapper. Legal form
can vary. Privileged treatment requires beneficial-control attestation.

This mirrors the economic-substance principle already present in this
framework: assets held through trusts, partnerships, or other structures
are attributed to the beneficial holder. Economic ownership rather than
legal form determines threshold treatment.

## Revenue and Capital Recycling

The dynasty tax generates revenue that flows through the PCA wind-down
architecture into explicit, accountable public capital. Unlike the
general fund treatment of estate tax revenue under the current system,
the wind-down capital is subject to PCA portfolio discipline: defined
deployment objectives, performance measurement, and sunset provisions.
It does not disappear into undifferentiated spending. It is invested
in the public systems — infrastructure, workforce development, research
capacity, the superannuation seeder — that contributed to the
appreciation being recognized.

This is the fiscal through line that makes the system coherent: the
capital recovered from closing the generational transfer escape hatch
is the capital that funds the universal stake every American child
receives at birth. The dynasty obligation above the threshold funds
the national gift below it. The threshold faces two directions, and
both directions are funded by the same mechanism.
