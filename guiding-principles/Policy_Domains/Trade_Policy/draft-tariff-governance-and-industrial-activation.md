# Tariff Governance & Industrial Activation Framework

## Executive Summary

This framework converts tariffs from a broad protectionist tool into a **targeted industrial coordination mechanism**.

Tariffs are not used as default policy.  
They are used **surgically**, to:

- Correct coordination failures  
- Enforce domestic capacity buildout where strategically required  
- Prevent premature collapse of emerging domestic industries  
- Align private investment decisions with national industrial priorities  

The system integrates directly with demand coordination, supplier activation, and corridor deployment systems.

---

## 1. First-Day Actions — Stabilization & Audit

On day one, the administration initiates a **full tariff system reset and classification process**.

### A. Industrial Input Exclusion List

All identified **critical industrial inputs** are placed into a temporary **tariff exclusion list**, including:

- Intermediate goods required for domestic manufacturing  
- Inputs identified through demand coordination system gaps  
- Critical materials not yet available domestically at scale  

This prevents:

> Tariffs from unintentionally suppressing domestic production by increasing input costs

---

### B. IDP Tariff Review

All tariffs applied to IDP partner nations are placed under immediate review:

- Evaluate impact on domestic producers  
- Identify cases where tariffs harm allied supply chain integration  
- Remove or modify tariffs that degrade IDP industrial capacity  

Objective:

> Align tariffs with alliance industrial integration, not undermine it

---

### C. Tariff Classification System

All tariffs are reclassified into three categories:

1. **Input-Suppressing Tariffs (to be removed)**
2. **Neutral Tariffs (to be evaluated)**
3. **Strategic Activation Tariffs (targeted, retained or deployed)**

---

## 2. Core Principle — Tariffs as Last-Step Enforcement

Tariffs are not the first move.

They are deployed only after:

1. Demand is visible  
2. Domestic or allied capacity is feasible  
3. Firms are given opportunity to invest  

This ensures:

> Tariffs enforce industrial outcomes rather than attempt to create them blindly

---

## 3. Industrial Activation Sequence

### Step 1 — Strategic Sector Identification

DoDA identifies sectors where:

- Domestic production is strategically required  
- Supply chain vulnerability is high  
- Demand is verifiable and sustained  

---

### Step 2 — Firm-Level Notification

Targeted firms (not countries) are notified:

- Identified dependency on external production  
- Domestic production opportunity  
- Available support (corridors, PCA, Execution Corps, supplier system)  

Clear timeline is established:

> X months to initiate domestic investment

---

### Step 3 — Deployment Support

If firms opt in:

- Execution Corps deploys into corridor  
- Supplier ecosystem activated via demand coordination system  
- PCA instruments reduce capital risk  
- Workforce pipelines initiated  

---

### Step 4 — Enforcement Trigger (Tariff Activation)

If firms do not respond within defined timeline:

- Targeted tariffs are applied to relevant imported goods  
- Tariffs are firm/product-specific where feasible  

This creates:

> A predictable, rules-based escalation rather than arbitrary trade action

---

## 4. Graduated Domestic Production Requirements

For designated strategic sectors:

### A. Market Share Thresholds

A defined percentage of domestic consumption must be supplied by:

- Domestic production  
- IDP-aligned production (where appropriate)

Example structure:

- Year 1–3: XX% domestic/IDP share required  
- Year 3–7: Increasing threshold  
- Long-term: Stable domestic-first baseline  

---

### B. Quota-Linked Tariff System

If thresholds are not met:

- Tariffs apply to excess imported goods beyond quota  
- Tariff intensity scales with deviation from target  

This creates:

> A continuous incentive to localize production without immediate shock

---

## 5. Circuit Breaker Integration

To prevent economic distortion or overreach:

### Automatic Safeguards

Tariffs are automatically reduced or suspended if:

- Domestic supply is insufficient  
- Prices exceed defined thresholds  
- Supply shortages emerge  
- Downstream industries are negatively impacted  

This ensures:

> Tariffs never become self-defeating constraints on the broader economy

---

## 6. Input Protection for Emerging Industries

New domestic industries are protected through:

- Temporary shielding from foreign competition  
- Gradual exposure to global markets  
- Integration into supplier ecosystem before tariff removal  

But:

- Protection is time-bound  
- Performance-based  
- Automatically sunset if targets are not met

## 8. Anti-Gaming Design

To prevent manipulation:

- Firms cannot offshore final assembly to bypass tariffs  
- Ownership and supply chain tracing required  
- IDP-origin goods treated differently from non-aligned sources  
- Tariffs apply at product-class level with firm-specific overlays where necessary  

---

## 9. What This Policy Is Not

This is not:

- Blanket protectionism  
- Anti-trade policy  
- Country-targeted tariff escalation  
- Permanent industry shielding  

It does not attempt to replace markets.

It corrects:

> Coordination failures that markets alone cannot resolve

---

## 10. System-Level Outcome

Tariffs become:

- Predictable  
- Targeted  
- Time-bound  
- Data-driven  

Instead of:

- Political  
- Reactive  
- Broad  
- Distortionary  

The system ensures:

> Domestic manufacturing grows because it is coordinated and supported  
> Not because imports are blindly restricted

# PUBLIC FACING DRAFT: Smart Tariffs That Actually Work

Right now, tariffs are often used like a hammer.

They hit everything — sometimes hurting American companies more than helping them.

This plan changes that.

We only use tariffs when they actually help build American industry.

First, we identify what we need to make at home.  
Then we give companies a real chance to invest here.  
We support them with infrastructure, workers, and financing.

Only if they choose not to act do tariffs kick in.

And even then, they’re targeted — not broad.

The goal is simple:

- Build more in America  
- Keep supply chains strong  
- Avoid unnecessary price increases  

Tariffs stop being political tools.

They become part of a smart, coordinated economic strategy.
