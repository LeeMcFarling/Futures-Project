## Domain Overview

**Purpose:**
Sports and cultural institutions as instruments of national capacity, hemispheric integration, community resilience, and cultural equity. The domain covers: sports governance and access architecture (making elite and recreational sport accessible and competitive); cultural product equity (ensuring commercial value from cultural goods routes back to source communities); and cultural institution design (national prestige, Olympic competitiveness, community identity infrastructure). This domain runs on the same anti-capture and anti-extraction logic as the rest of the platform — applied to sports governance and cultural economies rather than financial or pharmaceutical markets.

**Key Questions:**
- How do we restore access to sports participation across income levels without making it a government program?
- What makes Norway consistently dominant in the Olympics despite having 5.3 million people — and can that model be adapted?
- How do we allow cultural products to be celebrated and commercialized without the extractive appropriation-without-benefit pattern that has characterized cultural industries?
- What hemispheric sports governance architecture serves US soft power and regional integration simultaneously?

**Success Criteria:**
- Pan-American Champions League governance architecture ratified and operationalized
- Operation: Save Hockey pilot active in at least one US-Canada corridor
- Norwegian Sports Culture Model adapted and piloted in 2-3 US contexts
- Cultural Surcharge / SSI-linked community benefit mechanism operational for at least one Native American and one urban cultural community context

- **Operation: Save Hockey** is the most immediately tractable brief — PCA rink financing + cooperative equipment purchasing is straightforward infrastructure + cooperative economics; US-Canada joint instrument inside NORAM framework; the talent pipeline argument (hockey becoming a sport for wealthy families only) is the strongest political entry point
- **Norwegian Sports Model** is the most transformative structurally — shifting from school-based to club-based sports culture changes who has access to athletic development regardless of school budget; public facility investment + no early competition + cross-training are the three key design elements
- The domain sits at the intersection of several platform themes: anti-extraction (cultural surcharge), anti-capture (PACL governance), public investment in access (rinks, facilities), and regional integration (NORAM hockey, hemispheric football)
