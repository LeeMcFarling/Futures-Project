# Workforce Training & Education Alignment

## Purpose

This brief defines how training and education systems are aligned with real labor demand.

Training is not a standalone system.  
It is a **demand-linked execution layer** activated through workforce coordination.

The objective is to ensure that:
- workers are trained for real jobs  
- training timelines align with hiring timelines  
- program outcomes translate directly into employment  

---

## Core Principle

Training is not supply-driven.

> It is activated only in response to certified labor demand and is tied directly to defined job pathways.

Programs exist to fill roles—not to generate credentials in isolation.

---

## System Role

Training operates as a **subsystem of workforce allocation**:

- **DoDA** identifies demand and skill gaps  
- **WCA** activates and coordinates training capacity  
- **ISA** verifies that training outcomes translate into lawful employment  

Education providers deliver training, but do not define demand.

---

## Workforce Activation Contracts (WACs)

Training capacity is activated through Workforce Activation Contracts.

Each WAC includes:

- number of training seats  
- skill requirements and curriculum alignment  
- start dates and duration  
- geographic location  
- associated job pathways and wages  
- placement targets  

Training providers commit to delivery.  
Employers commit to hiring aligned cohorts.

---

## Training Providers

WCA coordinates with a network of providers, including:

- community colleges  
- technical and trade schools  
- union apprenticeship programs  
- accredited private training providers  

These institutions serve as the **primary execution layer** for workforce training.

Universities participate in:
- advanced skill development  
- engineering and technical pathways  
- research-linked workforce pipelines  

They are not the primary throughput mechanism for short-cycle labor needs.

---

## Training Activation Logic

Training is activated when:

- demand is certified by WCA  
- domestic workforce capacity is insufficient  
- roles can be filled within training timelines  

Training is not activated when:

- roles can be filled immediately  
- timelines cannot accommodate training  
- demand is uncertain or unverified  

---

## Timeline Synchronization

Training is scheduled to align with:

- employer hiring timelines  
- facility ramp-up schedules  
- production start dates  

Cohorts begin:

> in advance of labor demand realization, not in response to shortages after they occur

---

## Curriculum Alignment

Curriculum is aligned to:

- specific roles and equipment  
- employer requirements  
- industry standards  

Programs are:

- modular  
- adaptable  
- continuously updated based on DoDA feedback  

Generic, non-aligned training programs are deprioritized.

---

## Performance Metrics

Training providers are evaluated based on:

- placement rates into certified roles  
- wage outcomes  
- completion rates  
- alignment with demand timelines  

Funding and future participation are tied to outcomes, not enrollment volume.

---

## Worker Pathways

Workers access training through Workforce Access Points.

They receive:

- clear pathway visibility  
- defined timelines  
- expected wages upon completion  
- direct linkage to employment  

Training is presented as:

> a pathway into a specific job, not an open-ended educational track

---

## Interaction with Immigration

Training is prioritized before immigration pathways are activated.

If training cannot meet demand within required timelines:

- WCA certifies residual gaps  
- immigration pathways are activated via ISA  

Training and immigration operate as complementary tools within the same system.

---

## Employer Integration

Employers participate by:

- validating skill requirements  
- aligning hiring timelines  
- committing to hiring trained cohorts  

Employers do not dictate training content independently.

All programs are:
> system-aligned and standardized where appropriate

---

## System Properties

When functioning correctly, the system produces:

- tight alignment between training and employment  
- reduced labor shortages  
- higher workforce participation  
- minimal reliance on immigration for trainable roles  
- improved wage stability  

---

## Guardrails

1. **No Independent Program Expansion**  
   Training programs cannot expand without certified demand.

2. **No Enrollment-Driven Incentives**  
   Funding is tied to outcomes, not headcount.

3. **No Curriculum Drift**  
   Programs must remain aligned to real job requirements.

4. **Full Traceability**  
   All training pathways are linked to certified labor demand and outcomes.

---

## Plain-English Explanation

Training is built around real jobs.

Instead of people going to school and hoping it leads to work, this system starts with the jobs that actually exist and builds training around them.

If there are 100 jobs that need to be filled, training programs are created or expanded to prepare people for those exact roles, with clear timelines and expected pay.

Workers know what they’re training for, when it starts, how long it takes, and what job it leads to.

Employers get workers who are trained for the work they actually need.

The system connects training directly to employment, instead of leaving them disconnected.
