# Workforce Access Points (WAPs)

## Purpose

Workforce Access Points provide a **single, physical and digital interface** through which workers, employers, and communities interact with the workforce, training, and labor mobility system.

They make the system:
- visible  
- navigable  
- actionable  

WAPs do not make policy decisions.  
They translate system state into real-world action.

---

## Core Principle

The system must be understandable at the point of use.

> Every worker and employer should be able to walk into a local access point and receive clear, immediate answers about jobs, training, and participation.

---

## Role in the System

WAPs function as the **interface layer** connecting:

- WCA (coordination and allocation)  
- ISA (lifecycle administration and compliance)  
- training providers  
- employers  
- Execution Corps deployments  

They present these components as a unified system.

---

## Functions

### 1. Worker Intake and Pathway Routing

Workers can:

- view available job pathways  
- enroll in training aligned to certified demand  
- understand timelines, wages, and outcomes  

WAPs provide:

- clear pathway visibility  
- enrollment support  
- transition guidance  

---

### 2. Training Enrollment Coordination

WAPs connect workers to:

- WCA-activated training programs  
- apprenticeship pathways  
- credential bridging programs  

All training is:

> tied to real job outcomes and defined timelines

---

### 3. Employment Matching

WAPs facilitate:

- direct placement into available roles  
- alignment with employer hiring timelines  
- transition from training to employment  

---

### 4. Immigrant Worker Onboarding Support

In coordination with ISA, WAPs ensure:

- workers understand their role, rights, and obligations  
- onboarding conditions are functioning  
- access to reporting and support systems  

WAPs do not enforce compliance, but they surface issues.

---

### 5. Employer Interface

Employers use WAPs to:

- understand available workforce pathways  
- align hiring with training timelines  
- resolve coordination issues  

Employer access remains subject to system rules.

---

### 6. Issue Resolution and Escalation

WAPs identify and route:

- training misalignment  
- employment mismatches  
- onboarding failures  
- worker concerns  

Issues are escalated to:

- WCA (allocation problems)  
- ISA (compliance issues)  
- Execution Corps (deployment issues)  

---

## Deployment Model

WAPs are established in:

- industrial corridors  
- major project zones  
- regions with active workforce activation  

They are co-located with:

- training providers (where possible)  
- local institutions  
- transportation and access hubs  

---

## Operating Model

WAPs operate under:

> centralized logic, local accessibility

They:

- read from system data (DoDA, WCA, ISA)  
- do not create or modify system rules  
- provide real-time information and routing  

---

## Constraints

WAPs:

- do not allocate labor independently  
- do not issue visas  
- do not enforce compliance  
- do not override system-defined pathways  

They are an interface, not an authority.

---

## System Effect

When functioning correctly, WAPs:

- reduce friction for workers and employers  
- increase participation in training pathways  
- improve alignment between labor supply and demand  
- make the system legible and trustworthy  

---

## Plain-English Explanation

Workforce Access Points are where the system shows up in real life.

You can walk in and get a clear answer:

What jobs exist, what training you need, when it starts, and what it pays.

If something isn’t working—training, a job, onboarding—they help identify the problem and connect you to the right part of the system to fix it.

They don’t make the rules.  
They help you move through them.
