# Workforce Coordination Authority — Execution Corps Deployment Model

## Purpose

This brief defines how WCA operates when embedded within Execution Corps deployments.

WCA does not change its authority in deployment.  
It changes its **operational proximity and response time**.

---

## Deployment Trigger & System Integration

WCA is activated as part of the **Execution Corps deployment bundle**.

At the moment a corridor or industrial project is designated for deployment, the Execution Corps instantiates a coordinated institutional stack, including:

- Workforce Coordination Authority (WCA)  
- Immigration Services and Administration (ISA)  
- Public Capital Authority (PCA)  
- Regulatory Modernization Corps (RMC)  
- Department of Data and Accountability (DoDA) support functions  

WCA is not deployed reactively.

> Workforce coordination is activated at the same time as capital allocation and regulatory processes, ensuring that labor, permitting, and construction timelines progress in parallel.

No industrial deployment proceeds without workforce coordination capacity in place.

---

## Role in Execution Corps Deployment

Within an active industrial deployment, WCA operates as the **labor synchronization unit**.

From the point of designation, WCA:

- ingests project labor models and hiring timelines  
- defines workforce requirements by role, skill level, and phase  
- establishes Workforce Activation Contracts (WACs)  
- activates training pipelines aligned to projected demand  
- coordinates domestic labor routing and mobility  

WCA operates in parallel with:

- regulatory processes (RMC)  
- capital deployment (PCA)  
- construction and project delivery (Execution Corps)  

Labor is not sequenced after these processes.

> Workforce readiness is developed concurrently to ensure that labor availability does not delay operational start.

It ensures that:
- workforce supply aligns with deployment timelines  
- training pipelines are activated ahead of demand  
- labor shortages do not delay production  

---

## Embedded Structure

WCA is deployed as part of a **Corridor Labor Cell**, alongside:

- Execution Corps (project delivery)  
- ISA (lifecycle administration)  
- local training providers  
- employer representatives  

WCA presence is:
- small  
- technical  
- continuously connected to central systems  

---

## Core Functions in Deployment

### 1. Real-Time Demand Validation

WCA continuously updates:

- hiring timelines  
- role-specific shortages  
- training throughput vs. need  

Adjustments are made without requiring new policy cycles.

---

### 2. Training Synchronization

WCA coordinates:

- cohort start dates  
- training capacity expansion  
- curriculum alignment to specific roles  

Training is tied directly to:
> live job pipelines, not projected demand alone

---

### 3. Workforce Routing

WCA directs workers into:

- immediate employment (if qualified)  
- training pathways (if needed)  
- relocation pathways (if geographically mismatched)  

This occurs through local Workforce Access Points.

---

### 4. Labor Gap Certification

When shortages persist:

- WCA confirms:
  - domestic pathways exhausted  
  - timelines cannot be met  

- triggers:
  → immigration pathway activation via ISA  

---

## Interaction with Employers

In deployment, WCA:

- works directly with employers on hiring timelines  
- aligns labor inflow with production ramp schedules  
- prevents:
  - over-hiring  
  - under-supply  
  - misaligned skill intake  

---

## Interaction with ISA

- WCA defines labor pathways  
- ISA executes and maintains them  

In deployment:
- coordination is continuous  
- handoffs are minimized  

---

## Constraints

WCA in deployment:

- does not issue visas  
- does not override system rules  
- does not operate as a local decision authority  

All actions remain:
> system-derived and centrally logged

---

## System Effect

When deployed correctly:

- labor shortages do not stall projects  
- training pipelines are precisely timed  
- immigration is minimal and targeted  
- workforce supply scales with production  

WCA ensures that labor arrives:
> at the right place, at the right time, with the right skills
