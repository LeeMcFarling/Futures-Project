## Domain Overview

**Purpose:**
Rebuild America's industrial capacity through corridor-based deployment, PCA capital instruments, allied industrial integration (IDP), and regulatory modernization — while mitigating the displacement and gentrification risks that follow industrial revivals in urban and post-industrial communities. Manufacturing is the domain where the platform's core instruments (PCA, Execution Corps, ISA, WCA, RMS) are most fully instantiated in operational detail.

**Key Questions:**
- Which corridors are viable and in what sequence do we activate them?
- How do we deploy PCA capital to crowd in private investment rather than substitute for it?
- How do we connect workforce supply (ISA + WCA) to industrial demand signals before factories open?
- How do we prevent manufacturing revival from displacing the communities it's supposed to serve?

**Success Criteria:**
- Industrial corridor viability criteria deployed and first pilot cohorts selected
- PCA manufacturing instruments (JV, supplier credit, cluster infrastructure) operational
- NORAM critical materials framework agreed trilaterally
- Permitting shot clock enacted and enforcement underway
- Workforce Coordination Authority and ISA Execution Corps deployment models operational
- Anti-displacement architecture (land value capture, Dutch disease mitigation) built into first-wave corridors

## Notes & Context

- Domain has **28 files** across 6 subdirectories totaling ~60,995 words — the most operationally detailed domain outside Healthcare
- **PCA manufacturing tooling is the most complete capital deployment architecture in the platform** — manufacturing-pca-instruments-framework.md (v0.2, ~4,578 words) covers the full instrument catalog; supplier-credit-facilities.md (~4,958 words) is the most detailed single instrument spec
- **Anti-displacement architecture is explicit and built-in, not retrofitted** — land-use-stabilization-and-inclusive-growth.md, localized-dutch-disease-mitigation.md, and land-value-capture-resident-equity.md together constitute a housing/equity protection stack for manufacturing revival areas; this is what separates the platform from prior industrial policy (CHIPS Act, etc.) which created displacement without remedy
- **IDP alliance industrial target map** connects manufacturing directly to National Security — this is the economic production arm of the IDP framework; alliance-scale co-investment and offtake compacts are the economic complement to the military integration framework
- **Regulatory modernization in Manufacturing is the most developed sector-specific RMS instantiation after Energy** — permitting shot clock (v0.2) has a semiconductor fab example; manufacturing-regulatory-modernization-sandbox.md and regulatory-modernization-deployments-v0.3.md provide the governance architecture
- **Workforce subdomain is thin** (~2,595 words across 4 files) relative to the capital and strategy subdomains — WCA and ISA deployment models exist but the pre-deployment pipeline connecting demand signals to workforce supply is not yet specified
- NORAM_Critical_Materials_and_Manufacturing.md is the bridge between Manufacturing and Trade_Policy — the NORAM near-shoring framework in Trade and the domestic materials architecture here are designed as a system
- **Domain was created as a standalone domain** (not decomposed from Economic_Prosperity) but had `domain: Economic_Prosperity` in its YAML — corrected to `domain: Manufacturing` on 2026-05-13
- Urban industrial integration overview is thin (~806 words) — this is the interface between Manufacturing and Housing_and_Public_Infrastructure; needs more depth as corridors approach real-site selection
