## Problem Statement

The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) and Child Tax Credit (CTC) are primarily delivered as annual lump sums.

While effective in aggregate redistribution, annual delivery fails to address month-to-month income volatility. Households facing irregular hours, wage variability, or seasonal employment experience liquidity stress during the year despite being eligible for credits.

This timing mismatch reduces the stabilizing effect of tax credits and weakens work incentives near eligibility bands.

## Policy Objective

Modernize EITC and CTC delivery by:

1. Providing optional real-time or periodic advance payments tied to current earnings.
2. Preserving annual reconciliation for accuracy.
3. Reducing income volatility without expanding structural benefit cliffs.

## Core Structural Reform

### Optional Advance Credit Delivery

Eligible households may elect to receive periodic (e.g., monthly or per-pay-period) advance payments of anticipated EITC and CTC benefits.

Advance amounts are calculated using:

- Real-time earnings data.
- Prior-year income baselines.
- Conservative estimation formulas to limit overpayment risk.

### Safe Harbor Reconciliation

Annual tax filing reconciles advance payments against final eligibility.

Safe harbor provisions limit repayment obligations for low-income households when estimation errors occur within defined thresholds.

Intentional misreporting remains subject to existing fraud statutes.

### Earnings Data Integration

Advance delivery relies on standardized earnings reporting and interoperable payment infrastructure aligned with SSI/UPI rails.

Large employers and payroll processors provide near–real-time income reporting.

Independent contractor income reporting is integrated under Worker Classification Parity reforms to prevent blind spots.

### Volatility Smoothing Design

Credit phase-in and phase-out structures remain unchanged unless separately amended.

This Act modifies delivery timing, not credit generosity.

Periodic delivery is optional; households may retain annual lump-sum distribution if preferred.

## Interaction with Benefits Gradient Modernization

Real-time credit delivery complements Benefits Gradient Modernization by smoothing disposable income across taper bands.

Together, the reforms ensure that:

- Additional work increases net income.
- Net income gains are distributed throughout the year rather than concentrated annually.

## Fiscal Considerations

No structural expansion of credit size is required.

Administrative investment in reporting and payment infrastructure is necessary.

Cash-flow timing of federal outlays shifts forward within the fiscal year but remains neutral over annual cycles.

## Evaluation Metrics

- Reduction in month-to-month disposable income volatility.
- Uptake rate of advance payment option.
- Reconciliation error rates.
- Labor participation in near-threshold income bands.
- Household liquidity indicators.

## Strategic Framing

This reform modernizes delivery timing without altering the underlying tax credit structure.

It converts annual redistribution into continuous income stabilization, aligning tax policy with real labor-market volatility.

The objective is predictability: earnings increase, net income increases, and support arrives when it is needed.
