## Executive Summary

The American tax code currently attempts to perform too many functions at
once. It raises revenue, distributes benefits, subsidizes favored sectors,
shapes industrial policy, rewards specific behaviors, and compensates for
failures elsewhere in the state. The result is a sprawling code that is
expensive to administer, difficult to understand, easy to game, and
structurally misaligned with long-run national priorities.

This framework restores tax policy to its proper role: raising public
revenue cleanly, predictably, and fairly.

Functions better handled elsewhere — industrial investment, household
stabilization, sectoral development, and targeted risk-sharing — should
migrate out of the tax code and into explicit operating institutions such
as the Public Capital Authority, direct transfer systems, or dedicated
program statutes.

The modern code makes one distinction that prior reform efforts have
failed to make explicitly: between broad-based wealth formation, which
remains fully rewarded, and permanent dynastic insulation at extreme
scale, which the system no longer accommodates. Below $50 million in
total assets, normal wealth-building incentives remain intact. Above
that threshold, the system introduces realization parity rules that
ensure capital of sufficient scale interacts with the tax system when
it is accessed — through sale, borrowing, or equivalent monetization
— rather than compounding indefinitely behind structural escape hatches.

The objective is not simply lower or higher taxes. It is a cleaner
public finance architecture in which the tax code does one job well:
raise revenue. Everything else is done by institutions built for it.

---

## Core Principle

The tax code should be a revenue system, not a substitute for governance.

Taxes should fund legitimate public obligations through transparent
collection mechanisms. They should not serve as a hidden spending system,
a permanent lobbying marketplace, or the primary vehicle for industrial
policy.

Where government seeks to invest, stabilize, subsidize, or accelerate
development, it should do so explicitly through accountable institutions
rather than through opaque tax preferences.

The code taxes income, profit, transfers, and monetization events. It
does not tax passive ownership merely for existing.

## Current Structural Failures

### Hidden Spending Through Tax Expenditures

Large portions of federal economic policy occur through deductions,
credits, exemptions, and preferential treatment that function
economically as spending but receive less scrutiny than direct
appropriations. This creates a shadow budget that is difficult to
measure, politically sticky, and weakly disciplined.

### Policy Accumulation Without Sunset

Temporary provisions are repeatedly extended. Legacy incentives remain
long after original conditions changed. Narrow carve-outs accumulate
faster than they are removed. The result is a code optimized for
historical bargains rather than present needs.

### Complexity as Regressive Subsidy

Large firms and high-asset households can arbitrage complexity through
sophisticated planning structures unavailable to ordinary taxpayers.
This is not a minor inefficiency. It is the mechanism through which
the most consequential escape hatches operate. The buy-borrow-die
structure — borrowing indefinitely against appreciated assets, never
selling, dying with a stepped-up basis — is not a loophole discovered
by clever lawyers. It is a structural consequence of a system whose
complexity is only navigable at scale. Complexity itself becomes a
subsidy for those with sufficient resources to exploit it, while
imposing compliance costs on everyone who cannot.

### Administrative Friction for Ordinary Citizens

Millions of households navigate annual filing burdens, delayed refunds,
fragmented credits, and inconsistent income verification systems despite
the government already possessing much of the necessary information.

### Misplaced Policy Instruments

Tax incentives are frequently used where direct capital tools, grants,
loans, procurement, or automatic transfers would be more precise and
more accountable. The cost of this misplacement is paid twice: once in
forgone revenue and once in outcomes that never materialize because the
instrument was never designed to produce them.

## Reformed Tax Code Objectives

A modern tax system pursues five objectives.

It raises revenue reliably, from a base that is broad, legible, and
difficult to avoid. Revenue is stable across business cycles and
sufficient to fund authorized obligations.

It minimizes distortion where possible, interfering as little as
practical with productive work, legitimate investment, and ordinary
household formation — while recognizing that all tax systems create
tradeoffs and that eliminating one distortion can introduce another.

It enforces realization parity at extreme scale, ensuring that above
the $50 million threshold, the method of accessing accumulated wealth
— sale, borrowing, or synthetic monetization — does not determine
whether the tax system applies. Monetization is economically equivalent
to realization at sufficient scale, and the code treats it accordingly.

It reduces compliance burden for ordinary households, who should
experience taxation primarily as automated withholding, pre-populated
reconciliation, and simple annual confirmation.

It separates revenue from investment policy. Where public capital
deployment is needed, the system uses explicit institutions and
appropriated authority rather than burying strategy inside the tax code.

## Realization Parity at Extreme Scale

The tax code has long distinguished between realized and unrealized
gains. That distinction is reasonable at ordinary scale: a small
business owner whose company has appreciated in value but who has not
sold it, a homeowner whose property has risen in price, a worker whose
401k has grown — none of these individuals have accessed the economic
value of that appreciation in a way that creates an obvious tax event.

The distinction becomes structurally indefensible at extreme scale,
where it enables permanent, frictionless insulation from the tax system
through strategies unavailable to ordinary taxpayers.

The mechanism is well understood. An individual holds a billion-dollar
appreciated position. Rather than sell — which would trigger immediate
tax — they borrow against it at low interest rates. The loan provides
cash. The asset continues to compound. At death, the basis steps up
and the heirs inherit the position free of the embedded gain. The
accumulated tax liability disappears entirely. This is not a tax
deferral strategy. It is a permanent elimination strategy, available
only to those with positions large enough to make perpetual borrowing
economically dominant over selling.

Realization parity rules address this by treating monetization events
— accessing the economic value of appreciated assets through borrowing
or equivalent instruments — as partial recognition events proportional
to the value accessed. The rules apply above the $50 million threshold.
Below it, existing treatment remains unchanged.

This is not a wealth tax. A wealth tax applies to static holdings and
creates obligations from ownership alone. Realization parity applies
to voluntary transactions — specific decisions to access accumulated
value. The obligation arises when the individual monetizes, not when
they hold. An individual who holds $5 billion in appreciated stock and
neither sells nor borrows against it incurs no obligation under this
framework. The moment they access that value — through any instrument
whose primary purpose or material effect is to convert appreciation
into liquidity — the system recognizes the transaction for what it
economically is.

The specific mechanisms through which realization parity operates are
established in the borrowing-triggered partial realization brief and
the dynasty tax and generational transfer framework. Together they
close the two primary paths through which extreme accumulated
appreciation has remained permanently outside the tax system: active
monetization through borrowing and synthetic instruments, and the
generational transfer through the step-up basis elimination that
currently resets embedded gain at death.

## The Anti-Dynasty Threshold Framework

$50 million in total assets is the regime boundary between
ordinary wealth-building policy and extreme capital-scale
anti-arbitrage policy.

That line is not arbitrary. It is set deliberately above the level
of successful entrepreneurship, professional accumulation, family
business continuity, and upper-middle-class wealth, and below the
level at which perpetual borrowing against appreciated positions
becomes a dominant economic strategy. Below the threshold, the full
upside of wealth creation remains intact. Above it, the system
introduces structured obligations designed to keep extreme accumulated
wealth connected to the public and economic environment that made it
possible.

The threshold does five things simultaneously. It exempts ordinary
affluence and upper-middle wealth from the framework entirely.
It targets true capital-scale actors — the population for whom
buy-borrow-die is a planning strategy rather than a theoretical
concept. It creates a common boundary across multiple instruments,
simplifying administration and preventing threshold arbitrage across
different provisions. It provides a moral logic: above this level,
the relationship between private fortune and public system changes
in a way that warrants structured recognition. And it does all of
this with a single number that is legible, defensible, and
administratively clean.

The instruments that operationalize the threshold — borrowing-triggered
partial realization and the dynasty tax and generational transfer
framework — are designed as complementary rather than redundant. The
borrowing trigger closes active monetization: accessing appreciated
value through debt or equivalent structures rather than sale. The
dynasty tax closes the generational transfer and the holding-forever
path simultaneously: death, or the 100-year mark for structures
designed to avoid a death event, is a realization event rather than
a basis-reset opportunity. Together they make permanent, frictionless
insulation from the tax system structurally unavailable at extreme
scale, without touching the wealth-building incentives that apply
below the threshold.

## What Leaves the Tax Code

Over time, the following functions migrate into dedicated systems:

Industrial subsidies move to Public Capital Authority instruments,
where they are deployed as explicit, accountable, recyclable capital
rather than off-budget preferences.

Household income smoothing moves to direct transfer rails and real-time
credit delivery, where it reaches people when they need it rather than
as an annual lump sum decoupled from actual cash flow.

Sector-specific risk sharing moves to revolving funds and loan
authorities, where the state can absorb early risk and exit as markets
mature.

Regional development incentives move to corridor and infrastructure
statutes, where they are tied to defined outcomes and subject to DoDA
certification.

Narrow behavioral carve-outs are subject to sunset review. Those that
cannot demonstrate measurable outcomes against the capital they cost
are repealed.

The tax code becomes narrower and more coherent as these functions
migrate. What remains is a revenue system that does its job well
because it is no longer being asked to do seven other jobs badly.

---

## What Stays in the Tax Code

The tax code remains responsible for income taxation, consumption and
transaction taxes where enacted, payroll and social insurance
collection, capital gains recognition rules including realization parity
at extreme scale, corporate profit taxation, anti-avoidance and
base-protection rules, and transparent household support mechanisms
where administratively efficient.

Household support mechanisms that primarily benefit ordinary families —
the mortgage interest deduction in its current form, the charitable
contribution deduction, child and dependent care credits — are not tax
expenditures in the relevant sense and are not targets of this
framework. The distinction being drawn is between provisions that serve
ordinary household formation and consumption and provisions that serve
as planning tools for extreme wealth concentration. The former remains
in the code. The latter is converted, sunset, or restructured.

## Administrative Modernization

A simplified tax code requires modern administration.

The IRS should operate as a digital execution instrument capable of
pre-populated filing for standard households, real-time income
attestation, automated reconciliation across credits and obligations,
fast refund and settlement processing, standardized employer reporting,
lower audit burden for compliant taxpayers, and higher detection
capability for sophisticated evasion.

Tax simplification without administrative modernization is incomplete.
A narrower code that cannot be administered cleanly produces a
different set of compliance failures than a complex code. The
administrative modernization agenda runs in parallel with the
structural reform agenda, not after it.

The enforcement architecture for realization parity at extreme scale
operates through Treasury classification authority, DoDA continuous
monitoring, and a time-bounded Execution Corps phase-in instrument
that stands up reporting infrastructure before sunsetting. This
architecture is detailed in the borrowing-triggered realization
enforcement brief and applies the same institutional logic — classify,
monitor, measure, sunset — that governs the broader government
operating system.

## Revenue Logic

This framework does not rely on a single rate increase or one-time
windfall. Revenue arises through a combination of mechanisms that
compound over time rather than front-loading a political bet on any
single provision.

Broader and cleaner bases produce more reliable revenue than narrow
bases with high rates and large carve-outs. Reduction of inefficient
tax expenditures recovers forgone revenue that currently flows to
rent-seeking rather than productive activity. Improved compliance and
lower leakage — through administrative modernization and the immutable
ledger's attribution infrastructure — recovers revenue that was
authorized but not collected. Realization parity rules recognize gains
when economically accessed through sale, borrowing, or equivalent
structures at extreme scale, ending the permanent elimination strategy
that currently operates through buy-borrow-die. Simplified withholding
and reconciliation systems reduce the friction that causes under-
collection at the household level. Higher long-run growth from more
efficient capital allocation — with public investment moving through
the PCA rather than through distorting tax preferences — expands the
base over time.

No single one of these produces transformative revenue on its own.
Together they reorient the system toward durable collection from a
broad, legitimate base rather than episodic collection from a narrow,
engineered one.

## Relationship to Public Capital Authority

The PCA is the companion institution to tax reform. The tax code
collects revenue. The PCA deploys capital strategically. Separating
these functions allows each system to specialize.

Tax administration focuses on collection, compliance, and base
protection. Capital administration focuses on investment outcomes,
risk absorption, and recycling. This is superior to asking one
sprawling tax code to perform both functions badly by embedding
investment strategy in collection machinery.

Revenue recovered through the conversion of tax expenditures to PCA
instruments does not disappear. It becomes explicit capital deployed
through accountable instruments with defined outcomes, recycled when
those outcomes are achieved, and retired when they are not. The
discipline that applies to the collection side — broad base, clean
rules, enforceable obligations — applies equally to the deployment
side through the PCA's performance measurement and sunset architecture.

## Transition Strategy

Reform proceeds in phases that match the institutional capacity
available at each stage.

In the first twenty-four months, the priority is inventory and
infrastructure. Treasury conducts a comprehensive mapping of tax
expenditures by sector, objective, cost, duration, and beneficiary
concentration. Filing is simplified for standard households. IRS
reporting modernization begins. Temporary provisions undergo sunset
review. The PCA operating framework is established. The covered asset
and obligation reporting infrastructure required for realization
parity enforcement is stood up by the Execution Corps phase-in
instrument.

From years two through five, conversion and integration proceed.
Selected expenditures convert to PCA instruments in order of sector
readiness and beneficiary concentration. Automated reconciliation
systems expand. Legacy carve-outs narrow. Real-time credit delivery
integrates with SSI and UPI rails. The realization parity rules
operate under full enforcement with DoDA monitoring active.

From year five forward, the mature system operates with a narrow
revenue-focused code, full administrative automation for standard
taxpayers, and a dynamic anti-avoidance architecture for complex
structures that updates on the classification timescale the enforcement
brief establishes rather than the legislative timescale that complex
avoidance has historically outpaced.

## Strategic Framing

A healthy state taxes clearly, spends transparently, invests
deliberately, and administers competently. The current code often does
the opposite: taxes opaquely, spends invisibly, invests accidentally,
and administers unevenly.

This reform restores proper institutional roles. The tax code becomes
a revenue system. Public investment becomes explicit. Household support
becomes timely. Avoidance at extreme scale becomes structurally
unavailable rather than merely discouraged.

The question has never been whether taxes should be high or low in the
abstract. The question is whether the tax system is performing jobs it
was never designed to perform, while failing the jobs only it can
perform. This framework answers by narrowing the code to revenue,
fairness, and enforceability — and building the institutions that
handle everything else.
