## Purpose

This brief establishes how labor immigration operates once domestic workforce pipelines are fully utilized and verified shortages remain. Labor immigration under this framework is not open-ended and not demand-blind. It is a controlled mechanism for filling specific labor gaps that cannot be met domestically, while maintaining strict border control and system legitimacy.

## When labor immigration is authorized

Labor immigration is authorized only after domestic labor forecasting confirms persistent shortages in defined sectors, skill levels, and regions. Immigration caps and visa volumes are downstream outputs of the workforce forecasting system and are reviewed on a fixed cadence.

No labor visa category exists independently of demonstrated demand. All authorizations are temporary, capped, and tied to specific economic needs.

## Visa structure and scope

Labor visas issued under this framework are:

- Time-bound, with defined start and end dates
- Sector-specific and region-specific
- Issued only for roles with verified shortages
- Subject to wage floors tied to local labor markets

Visas are not transferable outside approved sectors or regions without reauthorization. This preserves labor targeting while allowing limited mobility within approved parameters.

## Pre-vetting, placement, and transport

Workers are pre-vetted, background checked, and matched to verified employers before entry into the United States. Employment terms, location, duration, and compensation are known in advance.

Transport is coordinated directly to destination communities to eliminate arrival uncertainty, reduce exploitation, and prevent informal job-seeking networks.

## Restoring Short Term Labor Migration

Circular migration is an explicit design goal. Workers who complete their authorized term and depart on schedule are eligible for fast re-entry through priority processing in subsequent seasons or cycles.

Compliance is rewarded with speed. Non-compliance results in immediate loss of eligibility across programs. This incentive structure restores circular labor flows while dramatically reducing overstays.

## Employer accountability

Employers participating in labor immigration programs are subject to verification, bonding, and audit requirements. Wage compliance, working conditions, and placement accuracy are monitored.

Employers with strong compliance records receive faster processing and expanded access. Repeat violators lose program eligibility.

## Enforcement outcomes

By making lawful participation faster, safer, and more predictable than unlawful entry, the system shifts incentives away from smuggling and exploitation.

Enforcement focuses on system integrity rather than mass apprehension. Border control becomes a downstream consequence of design rather than discretionary action.

## Interaction with other immigration pathways

Participation in labor immigration programs does not automatically confer permanent residence or citizenship. Labor visas are economic instruments, not settlement guarantees.

However, lawful participation and repeated compliance may serve as positive signals for eligibility under separate, explicitly defined immigration pathways, without automatic conversion.

## Scope and limits

This system is not amnesty. It does not legalize unauthorized entry retroactively. It does not bypass domestic workers.

It is a disciplined labor-matching system designed to meet economic needs while restoring border control, predictability, and public trust.

# PUBLIC FACING DRAFT: 

This program exists for one reason: to fill jobs that need to be filled when there are not enough workers available domestically.

The system starts by looking inward. If Americans or lawful residents can fill the jobs, they should. Only when clear shortages remain does this program open limited, temporary visas tied to specific jobs in specific places.

Workers do not arrive hoping to find work. Jobs are identified first. Workers are approved in advance, matched to employers, and sent directly to where the work is. Everyone knows the terms ahead of time: how long the job lasts, what it pays, and when the worker must leave or renew.

Following the rules makes the system work in a worker’s favor. People who complete their work and leave on time are eligible to come back quickly in the future. People who break the rules lose access. This makes legal participation safer, faster, and more reliable than illegal entry.

This is not an open-border policy and it is not amnesty. The border is closed. The only way to participate is through legal channels. By making legal work pathways clear and predictable, the system reduces illegal crossings, weakens smuggling networks, and gives employers the labor they actually need.
