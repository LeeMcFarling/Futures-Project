## Purpose

Immigration systems fail when enforcement is applied only to workers and not to the
employers who create demand. This brief defines binding employer obligations that apply
uniformly across labor migration and student-to-industry pathways. This also closes the loop 
and ensures that immigration demand is legitimate. 

The objective is simple: legal immigration must not depress wages, enable abuse, or
reward firms that bypass labor standards.

## Core principle

If an employer benefits from access to immigrant labor, that employer accepts higher
accountability.

Compliance is a condition of participation, not an afterthought.

## Employer eligibility requirements

Employers participating in immigration programs must:

- Register roles in advance through authorized systems
- Demonstrate alignment with verified labor shortages
- Meet or exceed wage floors tied to local labor markets
- Comply with all applicable labor, safety, and tax laws
- Maintain accurate employment and payroll records

Eligibility is reviewed continuously, not only at entry.

## Wage floors and labor protections

All roles filled through immigration pathways are subject to wage floors indexed to
local prevailing wages.

Immigrant labor may not be used to undercut domestic workers or suppress compensation.
Benefits, hours, and working conditions must be equivalent to those offered to
similarly situated domestic employees.

## Bonding, audits, and penalties

Participating employers are subject to:

- Bonding requirements proportional to workforce size
- Randomized and targeted audits
- Escalating penalties for violations

Penalties include fines, back pay, restitution, loss of program access, and referral for
civil or criminal enforcement where appropriate.

## Employer rating and access tiers

Employer compliance histories are tracked and scored.

- High-compliance employers receive expedited processing and expanded access
- Neutral employers receive standard access
- Repeat violators are barred from participation

This creates a market incentive for lawful behavior.

## Worker portability safeguards

To prevent coercion and abuse, workers are granted limited portability within approved
sectors and regions.

This ensures that employers cannot use visa status as leverage while preserving labor
matching integrity.

## Integration with enforcement infrastructure

Employer compliance is monitored through SSI-linked systems that integrate job
registration, payroll, tax withholding, and authorization status.

Violations are detected through data consistency rather than complaint-driven
enforcement alone.

## Plain-English explanation

If a company wants access to immigrant workers, it has to play by the rules.

That means paying fair wages, following labor laws, and treating workers the same way
they treat everyone else. Companies that do this well get faster access to talent.
Companies that cheat lose access.

This protects workers, protects wages, and ensures that immigration supports a fair and
competitive economy instead of rewarding bad actors.

Strong labor standards are how this system stays humane, credible, and sustainable.
