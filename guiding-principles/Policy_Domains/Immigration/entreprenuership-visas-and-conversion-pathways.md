## Core principle

Entrepreneurship visas exist to create net new U.S. productive capacity and jobs, not to
serve as an alternative labor visa or a loophole for extended stay.

Speed is permitted. Abuse is not.

---

## Entrepreneurship visa pathways

### Track A: Startup founders

For founders building venture-scale or high-growth companies.

Eligibility signals include:
- Capital commitment from a certified sponsor (VC fund, accelerator, or incubator)
- Acceptance into a recognized accelerator program
- Demonstrated revenue traction

### Track B: Small-business founders

For founders starting locally rooted businesses in trades, services, manufacturing,
care work, or other non-venture sectors.

Eligibility signals include:
- Capital deployment
- Local demand validation
- Licensing and permitting pathway

Both tracks are subject to the same integrity and compliance framework.

---

## Certified sponsors and fast processing

VC funds, accelerators, and incubators may apply to become **certified sponsors**.

Sponsors:
- Attest that a pitch is serious and capital is committed
- Do not approve visas or control immigration decisions
- Are subject to audit, caps, and decertification

Immigration officers are embedded near major startup hubs to allow **pitch → verification
→ provisional processing** without outsourcing authority to private actors.

---

## Provisional founder status

Initial approval grants **provisional founder status** (6–12 months).

This status:
- Authorizes work only on the declared business
- Is non-renewable without milestone completion
- Terminates automatically if milestones are missed

Required milestones include:
- Incorporation and tax registration
- Capital actually deployed (not merely pledged)
- Bank account and payroll setup
- Evidence of hiring, revenue, or product deployment

---

## Short-term visa holders and founder conversion

Short-term visa holders may prepare a business (incorporation, banking, pitching) but may
not operate it as a substitute for their visa purpose.

A one-time **Founder Provisional Conversion (FPC)** is available if strict criteria are
met:
- Clean compliance history
- Verified identity and biometrics
- Capital deployment or revenue contracts
- Certified sponsor attestation where applicable

Only one conversion attempt is permitted.

---

## SSI + UPI operational requirement (provisional phase)

To prevent shell companies and labor substitution, any business used to justify founder
or provisional status must operate on **SSI + UPI rails during the provisional period**.

Requirements:
- Business entity linked to SSI (beneficial ownership verified)
- All payroll, founder draws, and material transactions conducted via UPI
- Active transaction history consistent with declared operations

Lack of economic activity or inconsistent flows results in automatic termination of
status.

---

## Abuse prevention: structural safeguards

### Preventing shell companies
- No UPI activity → no milestone credit
- Dormant entities automatically disqualified

### Preventing labor subcontracting
- Revenue concentration caps (e.g., no single client > 50–60%)
- Cryptographic detection of payment overlap with prior employers
- Founder compensation parity rules to prevent self-paid “jobs”

### Preventing sponsor capture
- Published sponsor-level statistics
- Caps on annual sponsorship volume
- Bonds or escalating fees for repeat failures
- Decertification authority for visa-mill behavior

### Preventing repeat attempts
- One-time conversion rule
- Cooling-off periods
- Automatic expiration on failure

## Integration and civic participation

Entrepreneur visa holders fall under Tier 2 or Tier 3 integration requirements.

They must:
- Pay taxes immediately
- Participate in civic literacy
- Engage locally through mentorship, chambers of commerce, or community institutions

Entrepreneurship does not exempt civic responsibility.

---

## Why this prevents abuse without surveillance

This system does not rely on constant monitoring, content inspection, or discretionary
investigations.

Instead, it uses:
- Verified identity (SSI)
- Verified economic reality (UPI)
- Objective milestone checks
- Automatic enforcement

No personal data beyond what is required for lawful operation is collected. Financial
flows are evaluated only for structural consistency, not purpose or ideology.

SSI + UPI requirements apply only during provisional periods and only where immigration
status is justified by business activity. Mature businesses transition to standard
compliance regimes.

The result is a system that:
- Makes fraud structurally difficult
- Reduces human discretion
- Preserves privacy
- Maintains speed for legitimate founders

## Plain-English explanation

If someone wants to start a business here, we make it fast and clear—but we verify that
the business is real.

Founders can pitch an idea, get serious backing, and be processed quickly. But the
company has to actually operate, pay taxes, and create value. Paper companies and fake
startups don’t qualify.

We don’t spy on people. We just require that real businesses behave like real businesses.

That’s how you welcome entrepreneurs without opening the door to scams.
