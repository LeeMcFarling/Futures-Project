## Purpose

International students educated in the United States represent a high-signal talent
pool. They have already been screened, trained to U.S. standards, and integrated into
American academic and professional environments. Retaining this talent where it is
needed strengthens domestic industry without expanding uncontrolled immigration.

This brief establishes a narrow, enforceable pathway for students to transition from
education to employment only where verified labor shortages exist.

## Eligibility and scope

The pathway applies only to students who:

- Completed an accredited U.S. degree or credential  
- Maintained lawful status throughout their program  
- Seek employment in a sector with verified labor shortages  
- Accept employment that meets defined wage and location requirements  

No automatic or open-ended status is granted. Eligibility is conditional and time-limited.

## Time-bound job search window

Graduates are granted a defined, non-renewable window to secure qualifying employment.
During this period, work authorization is limited to approved shortage sectors.

Failure to secure qualifying employment within the window triggers departure. This
preserves enforcement credibility and prevents status drift.

## Conversion to work authorization

Upon securing qualifying employment, student status converts to a lawful work visa
tied to the approved role, sector, and region. Duration limits, wage floors, and
compliance rules mirror those applied to other labor migration programs.

This conversion is administrative rather than discretionary, reducing uncertainty
and processing delays.

## Employer requirements

Employers hiring through this pathway must:

- Verify that the role aligns with published labor shortages  
- Meet wage floors and labor standards  
- Register placements in advance  

Employers with strong compliance records receive expedited processing. Violations
result in loss of eligibility.

## Enforcement and compliance

Compliance is tracked through secure identity and authorization systems. Lawful
completion of employment terms preserves eligibility for future immigration pathways.
Non-compliance results in immediate loss of status and program access.

The objective is predictability: clear timelines, clear outcomes, and minimal discretion.

## Interaction with broader immigration system

This pathway does not guarantee permanent residence or citizenship. It is a workforce
retention mechanism, not a settlement program.

However, lawful participation and continued compliance may be considered positively
under separate, explicitly defined immigration pathways.

## Plain-English explanation

Students who are educated in the United States and have skills the country needs should
have a fair, orderly chance to work here. Especially after they have participated in a publically funded program or University. 

Under this system, graduates get a short, clearly defined period to find a qualifying
job in a field where workers are genuinely needed. If they succeed, they can stay and
work under clear rules. If they do not, they are expected to leave.

There are no open-ended stays and no automatic guarantees. The rules are the same for
everyone, and following them makes outcomes predictable for students, employers, and
the public.

This keeps talent the country has already trained, without weakening border enforcement
or public trust.
