## Core principle

Immigration is not complete at entry. It is complete when a person can function
independently, lawfully, and civically within the United States.

Integration is therefore a formal phase of lawful presence, not an optional add-on and
not a cultural test. Expectations scale with duration of stay and likelihood of long-term
residence.

---

## Tiered integration model

Integration requirements are proportional. Short-term presence requires functional
integration. Longer-term residence requires deeper civic participation.

### Tier 1: Short-term labor visas (≤ 12 months)

**Objective:** safety, functionality, and lawful participation.

Required pillars:
1. **Survival English**
   - Workplace communication
   - Emergency services
   - Housing, transport, healthcare basics

2. **Workplace and legal orientation**
   - Wage rights and labor protections
   - Tax withholding basics
   - Reporting abuse
   - Departure and renewal rules

3. **Community touchpoint (lightweight)**
   - Mandatory orientation session with a local provider
   - Introduction to a designated community liaison
   - Participation in at least one approved local briefing or event

There is no expectation of cultural assimilation or permanence. The goal is safe work,
lawful presence, and timely exit or renewal.

---

### Tier 2: Medium-term visas (2–5 years, student-to-work, refugee placements)

**Objective:** economic self-sufficiency and local stability.

Required pillars:
1. **Functional-to-proficient English**
   - Progressive benchmarks tied to renewal

2. **Economic integration**
   - Employment or credential bridging
   - Tax compliance
   - Job mobility within approved bounds

3. **Civic literacy**
   - How local, state, and federal government function
   - Labor law, tenant rights, and civic responsibilities

4. **Structured community participation**
   Participation is required but defined locally, within federal bounds.

   Acceptable mechanisms include:
   - Enrollment in a local civic organization, labor union, faith-based group, or
     volunteer association
   - Participation in a certified mentorship or buddy program
   - Completion of a minimum number of community-service hours
   - Attendance at approved community forums or local boards as observers

   The requirement is participation, not belief, ideology, or social outcome.

---

### Tier 3: Long-term residence and permanent pathways

**Objective:** full civic integration and institutional literacy.

Required pillars:
1. **English proficiency**
2. **Civic participation**
   - Completion of a standardized civics course
3. **Community anchoring**
   - Ongoing engagement with local institutions
4. **Legal and social stability**
   - Clean criminal record
   - Long-term tax compliance
   - Stable residence history

This tier aligns with naturalization norms without guaranteeing citizenship.

---

## Integration contracts

Each individual admitted under multi-year pathways enters into a written integration
contract at placement.

The contract specifies:
- Required integration pillars by tier
- Benchmarks and timelines
- Available supports
- Verification methods
- Consequences for non-participation
- What successful completion unlocks (renewal eligibility, re-entry priority, pathway
  progression)

Integration is measurable, enforceable, and transparent.

---

## Jurisdiction-defined participation

States, counties, and municipalities opt in to receive placements and define which
community participation mechanisms they will recognize, from a federally approved menu.

Jurisdictions retain control over:
- Qualifying organizations
- Participation thresholds
- Verification methods
- Alignment with local priorities

In exchange, jurisdictions receive:
- Per-capita integration funding
- Predictable placement volumes
- Reduced social friction
- Clear accountability mechanisms

There is no silent dumping and no unfunded mandate.

---

## Support infrastructure

Federal funding supports:
- English language instruction
- Credential evaluation and skills translation
- Job placement and apprenticeships
- Local integration providers

Support scales with integration expectations.

---

## Data and accountability

Integration outcomes are tracked and published separately from intake numbers.

Metrics include:
- Language progression
- Employment and wage outcomes
- Tax participation
- Program completion rates
- Compliance and attrition

Transparency ensures integration strengthens communities rather than eroding trust.

---

## Plain-English explanation

If someone is going to live in the United States for more than a short time, they are
expected to learn the language, work lawfully, follow the rules, and participate in the
community they live in.

We help people do this—but we also require it.

Communities decide what participation looks like locally: volunteering, mentorship,
civic meetings, unions, faith groups, or other local institutions. We don’t judge beliefs.
We require participation.

That’s how immigration strengthens the country instead of dividing it.
