# Federal Oversight and Impeachment Reform

## Summary
This proposal creates a fair, depoliticized, and automatic process for removing federal officials who face credible evidence of criminal misconduct.  It replaces partisan gridlock with a clear, two-stage system led by an independent Special Counsel and an Oversight Commission, ensuring that members of Congress and Executive Branch officials are held to the same legal standards as every citizen.


## Rationale
The U.S. impeachment system has devolved into a partisan weapon, often leaving clearly corrupt officials in power due to party loyalty.  This reform restores faith in government by establishing a transparent, rule-based mechanism that prioritizes integrity over factional control.

 “No person shall exercise public office under credible investigation for criminal acts committed in that office, pending an independent review by the Oversight Commission.”

---

## Stage 1: Special Counsel & Probable Cause Suspension
1. A **Special Counsel** is appointed by an **Independent Oversight Commission** when credible evidence arises against a sitting member of Congress or Executive Branch official.  
2. Within **90 days**, the Counsel must determine whether **probable cause** exists that a crime was committed.  
3. Upon certification of sufficient evidence, the official is **automatically suspended from duty** pending trial.  
   - Suspension is administrative, not punitive.  
   - The official retains pay and legal presumption of innocence.  
   - Acting authority passes to the lawful deputy to ensure continuity of governance.  

This stage protects government integrity without compromising due process.

---

## Stage 2: Judicial Trial & Final Conviction
1. If convicted in federal court, **permanent removal from office** occurs automatically by operation of law.  
2. Appeals are expedited through a **fast-track judicial panel** and may go directly to the Supreme Court within 60 days.  
3. Congress may, by **two-thirds supermajority**, vote to reinstate an official later proven innocent or wrongfully convicted.  

---

## Coverage
This process applies equally to:
- Members of **Congress** (House and Senate)  
- **Executive Branch** officials, including the President, Vice President, Cabinet members, and agency heads  
- **Appointed federal officials** confirmed by the Senate  

---

## Balance of Powers Clause
To maintain democratic flexibility and prevent judicial overreach, **Congress retains the authority** to expand or clarify the list of impeachable offenses.  
Such expansions require a **two-thirds vote in both chambers** and must be accompanied by a **public rationale**.  
Examples of additional impeachable offenses may include:
- Systemic misuse of public funds  
- Abuse of intelligence access for personal or political gain  
- Refusal to comply with lawful oversight subpoenas  
- Repeated ethics violations  

This clause ensures that integrity standards evolve with new forms of corruption while preserving constitutional balance.

---

## Timeline Safeguards
1. **Suspension within 90 days** of Special Counsel findings.  
2. **Fast-Track Judicial Panel** completes trial within 180 days.  
3. **Appeals limited to 60 days** and handled directly by the Supreme Court.  
4. **Automatic removal upon conviction**, with reinstatement possible only via appeal or congressional supermajority.  

---

## Global Precedents
Several democratic nations already implement similar mechanisms:  

- **Germany**: Independent judiciary handles removal of officials upon conviction.  
- **South Korea**: National Assembly impeachment is validated by the Constitutional Court within months, not years.  
- **France**: Special judicial panels try high officials outside partisan channels.  
- **Sweden**: Ombudsman investigations allow citizens to trigger misconduct inquiries directly.  

*Lesson:* Accountability strengthens democracy when enforcement is fast, fair, and insulated from party politics.
