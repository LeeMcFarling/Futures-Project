# Impeachment Reform — Oversight Commission Architecture and Special Counsel Framework

## Purpose

The Oversight Commission is the institutional backbone of the impeachment enforcement system. It governs the appointment, conduct, and accountability of the Special Counsel; receives and reviews probable cause determinations; activates the suspension mechanism; and ensures that enforcement is structurally independent of the political actors it is designed to hold accountable.

This brief establishes the Commission's composition, appointment process, conflict-of-interest rules, operational mandate, and relationship to the Special Counsel and the broader impeachment framework.

The Commission does not make political judgments. It administers a rule-bound system. Its legitimacy derives entirely from its independence, its transparency, and the precision with which it applies defined legal standards to established facts.

---

## Core Design Principle

The central failure mode of every prior accountability mechanism is captured independence. Investigative bodies are appointed by the powerful, staffed by the loyal, and constrained by the political relationships of those who fund and authorize them.

This framework is designed around a single governing insight: **no actor with a stake in the outcome may hold authority over the process.**

That principle is not aspirational. It is structural. Every appointment mechanism, conflict rule, and operational constraint in this brief exists to operationalize it.

## I. The Oversight Commission

### Composition

The Oversight Commission consists of seven members:

- **Two members** appointed by the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court from a pool of retired federal appellate judges with no current political affiliation
- **Two members** appointed by the American Bar Association's Standing Committee on Ethics and Professional Responsibility from a pool of senior legal practitioners with no current partisan role
- **One member** appointed by the National Academy of Sciences from a pool of scholars in constitutional law, political science, or institutional design
- **One member** appointed by a supermajority of state attorneys general, rotating on a defined schedule
- **One member** appointed by the Comptroller General of the United States from senior career officers of the Government Accountability Office

No member may be:

- a current or former elected official at any level of government
- a current or former political appointee of any administration within the preceding ten years
- a registered lobbyist or agent of a foreign government within the preceding ten years
- a current donor to any federal political campaign or party within the preceding four years
- a current or former employee of any entity that is or has been the subject of federal investigation within the preceding five years

### Terms

Members serve single, non-renewable terms of six years. Terms are staggered so that no more than two seats expire in any two-year period. Members may not be removed except for cause, defined as criminal conviction, incapacity, or demonstrated material conflict of interest, and only through a unanimous vote of the remaining members plus judicial confirmation of the removal basis.

### Chair

The Chair is elected by the members from among themselves by majority vote at the commencement of each two-year period. The Chair holds no additional authority over substantive determinations but manages Commission operations, public communications, and Special Counsel liaison.

### Quorum and Decision Rules

A quorum requires five members. Substantive determinations — including Special Counsel appointment, probable cause review, suspension activation, and removal recommendations — require a supermajority of five of seven members. Procedural decisions require simple majority of those present.

All votes are recorded and published within 48 hours of the determination to which they relate.

## II. Conflict of Interest Framework

### Mandatory Disclosure

Upon appointment and annually thereafter, each member must publicly disclose:

- all financial interests, including holdings, investments, and liabilities
- all professional relationships within the preceding ten years
- all family members holding federal office, political appointment, or senior roles in entities subject to federal regulation
- any prior professional relationship with any individual who is or becomes a subject of proceedings before the Commission

### Recusal Standards

A member must recuse from any proceeding in which:

- the member has a direct or indirect financial interest in the outcome
- the member has a prior professional relationship with the subject official, their legal representatives, or their immediate family
- the member has made public statements regarding the subject official's guilt or innocence within the preceding two years
- a reasonable observer would question the member's impartiality based on disclosed relationships

Recusal decisions are made by the member themselves and are subject to challenge by any other member. Disputed recusal questions are resolved by the remaining members by majority vote. In cases where recusal reduces the Commission below quorum, the appointing body for the recused member's seat appoints a temporary replacement from the eligible pool for the duration of the proceeding.

### Post-Service Restrictions

Members may not, within five years of service:

- represent any party in proceedings before the Commission or related courts
- accept employment with any entity that was the subject of Commission proceedings during their term
- engage in partisan political activity including campaign contribution, endorsement, or candidacy

## III. The Special Counsel

### Role and Mandate

The Special Counsel is an independent investigative and prosecutorial officer appointed by the Oversight Commission to conduct investigations and prosecutions arising under the enumerated trigger framework.

The Special Counsel is not an employee of the Department of Justice. The Special Counsel is not subject to the direction, supervision, or removal authority of the Attorney General, the President, or any other executive branch official. The Special Counsel's authority derives entirely from the Oversight Commission and is governed by this framework.

### Appointment

The Special Counsel is appointed by the Oversight Commission through the following process:

**Step 1 — Pool Construction**
The Commission maintains a standing pool of qualified candidates, updated annually, consisting of:

- retired federal judges with prosecutorial experience
- senior attorneys with no current partisan role and at least fifteen years of federal litigation experience
- former Inspectors General with demonstrated independence

Candidates are nominated by the Commission's appointing bodies and screened for conflicts under the standards defined in Section II.

**Step 2 — Selection**
Upon referral of a matter requiring Special Counsel, the Commission appoints from the standing pool by supermajority vote within fourteen days of referral. The selection is public and the basis for selection is documented.

**Step 3 — Scope Definition**
The Commission defines the investigative scope at appointment. Scope expansions require Commission approval by supermajority vote. The Special Counsel may not unilaterally expand the scope of investigation.

### Independence and Protection

The Special Counsel may not be removed except by the Oversight Commission, and only for:

- criminal conviction
- demonstrated material conflict of interest
- documented failure to conduct proceedings in accordance with this framework

Removal requires supermajority Commission vote plus judicial confirmation of the removal basis by a three-judge panel of the DC Circuit. No executive branch actor may initiate, direct, or contribute to removal proceedings.

Attempts by any executive branch official to interfere with, obstruct, or direct the Special Counsel's investigation constitute an impeachable event under trigger 3.1 of the enumerated triggers framework.

### Resources and Staffing

The Special Counsel has independent budget authority drawn from a standing appropriation that is not subject to executive branch impoundment or rescission. Staffing decisions are made by the Special Counsel without executive branch approval. Staff are subject to the same conflict-of-interest standards as Commission members for the duration of their appointment.

### Reporting

The Special Counsel reports to the Oversight Commission, not to Congress or the Executive Branch. Reports are transmitted to the Commission, which determines what is released publicly consistent with grand jury secrecy rules, classification requirements, and the privacy interests of individuals not charged.

Probable cause determinations are always public. Investigation methods, witness identities, and deliberative materials are protected until proceedings are concluded.

## IV. Probable Cause Determination Process

### Referral

A matter may be referred to the Special Counsel by:

- any member of Congress by written referral to the Commission
- the Comptroller General upon audit findings suggesting enumerated trigger conduct
- the Inspector General of any federal agency
- the Commission itself upon credible public information

Referral does not constitute a finding of probable cause. It initiates review.

### Review Timeline

Upon referral, the Special Counsel must:

- acknowledge receipt within 72 hours
- issue a preliminary assessment of referral sufficiency within 30 days
- complete the probable cause investigation and issue a determination within 120 days of a sufficient referral

Failure to meet these timelines results in automatic escalation to Commission review, which may appoint a replacement Special Counsel or issue a procedural finding.

### Probable Cause Standard

A finding of probable cause requires:

- identification of the specific enumerated trigger or triggers alleged
- clear and articulable evidence supporting a reasonable belief that the conduct occurred
- documented assessment that the safe harbor clause does not apply
- Special Counsel certification
- Commission supermajority confirmation

A finding of no probable cause is equally binding. It closes the matter unless new evidence emerges that was not available during the original review period.

### Activation of Suspension

Upon Commission confirmation of a probable cause finding, suspension of the subject official is automatic under the terms of the Suspension and Prosecution framework. The Commission notifies the relevant succession authority, the federal judiciary, and the public simultaneously and immediately.

No executive branch consent is required. No delay is permitted.

## V. Accountability and Transparency

### Public Reporting

The Commission publishes:

- an annual report on all referrals received, their disposition, and the timeline of each
- immediate public notice of all probable cause determinations and their specific trigger basis
- immediate public notice of all no-probable-cause findings
- quarterly disclosure of Commission member financial disclosures and any recusal events

### Congressional Oversight

The Commission appears before relevant congressional committees annually to report on operations. Commission members may not be compelled to testify regarding deliberative processes or specific pending investigations. They may be questioned regarding institutional operations, budget, and compliance with this framework.

Congress may not direct Commission investigative activity, alter Commission determinations, or remove Commission members through legislative action. Congressional oversight is limited to institutional accountability, not operational direction.

### Judicial Review

All probable cause determinations are subject to judicial review on petition of the subject official. Review is limited to:

- whether the determination identified a valid enumerated trigger
- whether the evidentiary standard was met
- whether conflict-of-interest requirements were followed
- whether procedural requirements were satisfied

Courts may not substitute their judgment on the merits of the underlying conduct for that of the Special Counsel and Commission. They may remand for reconsideration where procedural or evidentiary deficiencies are found.

Review is expedited. A determination must be issued within 30 days of petition. No stay of suspension is available pending review absent a judicial finding of a substantial likelihood of success on the merits.

## VI. Anti-Capture Mechanics

The Commission's independence is protected through layered structural constraints that operate without requiring any actor's good faith.

**Distributed appointment authority** ensures no single political actor controls a majority of seats. The appointing bodies — the Chief Justice, the ABA, the National Academy of Sciences, the state attorneys general, and the Comptroller General — have structurally distinct interests and constituencies.

**Staggered terms** prevent any single administration from appointing a Commission majority within a standard term of office.

**Post-service restrictions** eliminate the incentive to cultivate future employment relationships during service.

**Automatic escalation** on timeline failures ensures that delay cannot be weaponized.

**Judicial review with defined scope** provides a check on Commission error without providing a mechanism for political interference in Commission operations.

**Budget independence** eliminates executive branch leverage over Commission resources.

No single failure — a compromised member, a delayed investigation, a politically motivated referral — can collapse the system. Each safeguard operates independently. The system degrades gracefully under partial capture rather than failing completely.

---

## VII. Relationship to the Broader Impeachment Framework

The Oversight Commission and Special Counsel are the enforcement layer of a three-part system:

- **The Enumerated Triggers** define what conduct is impeachable
- **The Oversight Commission and Special Counsel** determine whether that conduct occurred
- **The Suspension and Prosecution Framework** enforces the consequence

Each layer is structurally independent. The triggers cannot be rewritten by the Commission. The Commission cannot be directed by the prosecution. The prosecution cannot be stayed by the executive.

The system is designed so that no single actor's failure or capture can prevent accountability from proceeding. That is not an accident. It is the central design objective.

---

## Conclusion

Every accountability system in American history has failed at the same point: the moment when enforcing the rules became politically costly for those with the power to enforce them.

This framework does not rely on political courage. It does not assume that powerful actors will choose accountability over self-preservation. It assumes the opposite — and builds a system that produces accountability regardless.

The Oversight Commission is not a body of heroes. It is a machine. It receives referrals, applies standards, makes determinations, and activates consequences. It does this on defined timelines, under public scrutiny, subject to judicial review, insulated from the actors it oversees.

It does not need to be brave. It needs to be independent, transparent, and structurally resistant to capture.

That is what this brief constructs.

And that is sufficient.
