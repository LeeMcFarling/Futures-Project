# Democratic Integrity Stack: Legislative Sequencing and Implementation Architecture

## Purpose

This brief defines the order of operations for preparing and implementing the Democratic Integrity Stack — from pre-office preparation through the constitutional amendment layer. It covers a trifecta scenario beginning in January 2029, with simple majority control of the House, Senate, and Presidency and no supermajority in either chamber.

It distinguishes what is achievable by statute, what requires the National Referendum Mechanism, and what requires constitutional amendment. It sequences each layer to maximize political viability, legal durability, and cascading reform momentum. It includes the legal pressure escalation doctrine under which court challenges can become pressure multipliers rather than stopping points — when the mandate is clear and the fallback path is ready.

This is not a theoretical framework. It is a legislative game plan for a defined political window, and a preparation plan for the years before that window opens.

---

## Scope Boundary

This brief sequences the **Democratic Integrity Stack only**. It does not sequence the Culture War Settlement Stack, Technology/Data Stack, Healthcare Stack, Industrial Strategy Stack, or the full Judicial Reform Stack.

Other domains may use Democratic Integrity instruments — especially the National Referendum Mechanism, Digital Democracy Platform, Candidate Nutrition Label, or public mandate architecture — but their substantive policy settlements are sequenced in their own domain sequencing briefs.

Culture-war settlement titles may serve as early NRM use cases or public ratification proof points, but they are not part of the Democratic Integrity Stack. They are referenced here only where they affect the timing, legal hardening, or public legitimacy of the NRM mechanism itself.

This brief is a domain sequencing document. It will eventually feed into a platform-wide Master Sequencing Document that coordinates legislative bandwidth, public mandate timing, institutional dependencies, and cross-domain implementation risk across the full Futures Project platform.

## The Constraint Set

**Trifecta with simple majority, no supermajority.** The majority controls the House, Senate, and Presidency. It does not have 60 Senate votes under current rules and does not have the two-thirds majorities required to propose constitutional amendments directly.

**The filibuster reform comes first.** Restoring the standing filibuster — requiring members who wish to block legislation to actively hold the floor — is itself a simple majority action. Under the precedent established by the Senate's own procedural rulings in 2013 and extended in 2017, a simple majority can change Senate procedure by ruling the current filibuster practice out of order. This requires 51 votes and a presiding officer who sustains the ruling.

The mechanics matter: the standing filibuster requires **continuous caucus floor presence**, not a single-senator endurance test. Senators can yield to each other for questions while retaining the floor, and extended filibusters have historically been conducted by rotating groups. The majority's primary tactical weapon is a quorum call, which forces the filibustering caucus to produce 51 senators or lose the floor. What the standing filibuster changes is the economics of obstruction — the minority must actively mobilize its entire caucus to maintain continuous presence on every bill it wants to block. That is a finite resource. It forces the minority to choose which fights are worth the cost rather than blocking everything at zero marginal effort under the current cloture rule.

**The constitutional boundary is real and non-negotiable.** The following elements require constitutional amendment and cannot be achieved by statute alone:

- Legislative term limits (House and Senate)
- Age limits for elected federal officials
- Electoral College abolition or fundamental restructuring
- Supreme Court hard tenure limits (statutory rotation bridge available in the interim)

Everything else in the stack is achievable by statute. That is a substantial majority of the reform agenda. The constitutional layer is the ceiling, not the floor.

**The NRM is both an immediate mandate tool and a long-run bridge.** In its lean first-round form, the NRM gets popular mandates on the record before the 2030 midterms and creates electoral pressure for implementing action. As it matures into higher tiers, it generates the constitutional mandate weight that makes Article V viable. The mechanism earns its institutional armor through demonstrated use, not pre-scheduled design.

**Legal challenges can become pressure multipliers when the fallback path is ready.** Every court challenge to a ratified mandate with strong public support creates an opportunity to escalate to a more durable implementation pathway — if the next lawful step is prepared in advance. The legal pressure escalation doctrine is documented in full below.

## Layer 0 — Pre-Office Legal Hardening, Technical Review, and Coalition Preparation

**Timing: 2026–January 2029.**

Layer 0 is the work conducted before the reform coalition holds governing power. Its purpose is to make the first governing year executable rather than theoretical. By January 2029, the coalition should enter office with legally reviewed statutory language, public-facing materials, external technical critique, early-adopter state readiness, NRM vessel testing, and organized constituencies prepared for political-market opening.

Layer 0 does not enact the stack. It prevents the first governing year from being consumed by drafting, legal uncertainty, and coalition discovery.

### International Technical Advisory and World-Stage Validity

The Democratic Integrity machinery should be submitted for external technical review before it becomes governing legislation. This includes the UN Electoral Assistance Division, EU election observation practitioners, Commonwealth electoral networks, comparative-election scholars, proportional representation administrators, independent electoral commission experts, and referendum-design practitioners.

This is not an internal source of legitimacy and does not transfer authority over American constitutional design. The purpose is technical review: to surface implementation risks, ballot-comprehension problems, proportional-representation tradeoffs, party-recognition failure modes, electoral commission capture risks, referendum-design weaknesses, and transition issues that other democracies have already encountered.

The United States retains full democratic responsibility for its own institutions. But democratic seriousness requires technical humility. The American constitutional-electoral machine was designed in the eighteenth century and patched through statute, doctrine, party custom, and state-level improvisation. Other democracies and international advisory bodies have helped build, observe, and repair modern electoral systems under contemporary conditions. Their critique should be solicited before the machinery enters the governing window.

The output is a public technical concern register, comparative-risk matrix, and revised draft language responding to major concerns.

### Constitutional and Statutory Legal Hardening

Before office, the reform coalition should obtain adversarial review of the NRM, FEIC authority, Representation Fairness, State Party Formation, Campaign Finance, Voter ID and Registration, Candidate Nutrition Label, and Article V mandate strategy.

The review should cover contingent-legislation doctrine, bicameralism and presentment, severability, sunset-on-failure, public ratification as a triggering condition, First Amendment campaign rules, Elections Clause authority, equal protection, Voting Rights Act risk, state administration constraints, Article V convention scope, and judicial review pathways.

The objective is not to eliminate litigation risk. The objective is to enter office with language already red-teamed by constitutional lawyers, election lawyers, congressional counsel, former judges, state attorney-general-style litigators, election administrators, and hostile readers. Legal challenge is central to the pressure doctrine. The legal review should happen before the first mandate fight, not after.

### NRM Vessel Testing Before High-Salience Use

Before the NRM is used for high-salience democratic reforms or constitutional mandate campaigns, the mechanism should be tested through mock ballots, public deliberation pilots, state-level advisory versions, or a narrow Public Ratification Pilot if a legislative vehicle exists before 2029.

The purpose is to test the vessel, not to transform policy. Can severable titles be written cleanly? Can voters understand the question? Can public ratification be framed as a statutory trigger rather than lawmaking? Can failed titles sunset without confusion? Can implementation pathways be written before the vote?

The first major use of the NRM should rely on reviewed language and tested public materials, not an untested theory introduced after office.

### Political-Market Mobilization

Layer 0 builds the constituency for political-market opening before the law changes. Minor parties, independents, anti-corruption groups, local reform coalitions, labor-populist formations, rural infrastructure coalitions, housing reformers, environmental parties, libertarian conservatives, localist conservatives, civic technologists, veterans' groups, and safe-district dissidents should understand the same proposition: the Democratic Integrity Stack creates a lawful path to representation without spoiler suicide.

The message is not that third parties win the presidency on day one. The message is that recognized state parties can earn representation, form congressional caucus blocs, build a governing record, and eventually roll into presidential caucus blocs. If the political market is not organized before the law changes, early adoption underperforms.

### Early-Adopter State Readiness

Layer 0 prepares states that may be willing to move first. Reform coalitions in early-adopter states should draft conditional legislation for certified electoral methods, including MMP, open-list PR, PR-STV, and certified independent districting. Election administrators and civic groups should model ballot design, tabulation procedures, audit pathways, voter education, party-recognition workflows, and representation heatmaps.

The goal is that early-adopter states are not starting from zero once federal legislation passes. By the time the governing window opens, they should have draft bills, administrative models, public simulations, and civic coalitions already organized around adoption. The 2030 early-adopter claims in later layers only work if this preparation happens before office.

### Public Demonstration Products

Layer 0 should produce public artifacts that translate the source-code architecture into visible democratic stakes:

- representation distortion heatmaps by state
- simulated delegations under MMP, open-list PR, PR-STV, and independent districting
- candidate nutrition label prototypes
- sample NRM ballot language
- state party recognition templates
- model Article V application language
- public mandate dashboards
- a duopoly distortion index
- a plain-language statutory/NRM/constitutional pathway map

These materials let voters see what the current system denies them and how each reform changes the incentive structure. The gap between source-code architecture and public comprehension must be closed before the governing window opens.

### Cross-Domain NRM Proof Points

The Culture War Settlement Stack is not part of the Democratic Integrity Stack and is sequenced separately. However, culture-war settlement titles may serve as early NRM proof points because they are designed around severable public activation: Congress writes the full law, discrete titles activate only upon public ratification, and failed titles sunset.

For Democratic Integrity purposes, their relevance is procedural rather than substantive. They test whether the NRM can decompose overloaded national conflicts into legible severable questions, generate public mandates, and discipline implementation — without requiring the Democratic Integrity electoral stack to carry the entire first-year burden.

### Layer 0 Success Condition

Layer 0 succeeds if, by January 2029, the coalition has:

- a legally reviewed NRM vessel
- external technical review of the Democratic Integrity machinery
- public response to major advisory concerns
- FEIC and Representation Fairness language in near-final form
- party formation and candidate-label language aligned with campaign finance and SSI requirements
- early-adopter state coalitions prepared for electoral method pilots
- public heatmap simulations showing representational distortion
- organized grassroots constituencies for political-market opening
- constitutional mandate machinery prepared for term limits, age limits, Electoral College reform, and SCOTUS active-service limits
- a clear distinction between Democratic Integrity sequencing, Culture War sequencing, and the future Master Platform Sequencing Document

## Layer 1 — Filibuster Reform

**Timing: January–March 2029.**

Before any substantive legislation, the Senate majority establishes the standing filibuster rule through a procedural majority vote. The presiding officer rules that requiring a supermajority to invoke cloture on ordinary legislation is inconsistent with the Senate's constitutional role as a continuing deliberative body. The ruling is sustained by simple majority. The standing filibuster rule is in effect.

The political case is made in advance and publicly: the standing filibuster was the historical norm for most of American history; the current cloture rule is a 20th-century procedural innovation that has been weaponized to block popular reforms with demonstrated supermajority public support; restoring it does not end debate, it requires participants in debate to actually participate.

This framing matters because the reform will be characterized as a power grab. The response is that the existing rule is itself a power grab by the minority — and that the reforms it has blocked poll at 60–80% public support across partisan lines.

## Layer 2 — Early Democratic Integrity Wins

**Timing: March–June 2029.**

These are the bills with the broadest public support, the weakest organized opposition, and the greatest capacity to build political momentum for harder fights. They are also the bills most likely to peel off individual opposition votes even under the standing filibuster — the political cost of publicly holding the floor to block a stock trading ban is prohibitively high.

**Ban Congressional and Executive Stock Trading.** Polls at supermajority levels across all partisan groups. The organized opposition is the members who trade stocks — a small and politically exposed group. Pass it first. It establishes the reform coalition's credibility on anti-corruption and creates immediate pressure on any member who obstructs.

**Election Day as a Federal Public Holiday.** Removes a structural participation barrier without changing any voting procedure, ballot design, or electoral administration. Opposition is weak and floor obstruction is politically costly.

**Voter ID and Registration Reform.** Universal automatic registration at point of government contact, SSI identity infrastructure deployment, and bridge provisions for existing government-issued ID. This preempts the "voter fraud" attack on the rest of the stack — it upgrades both access and security simultaneously. Pass it early so SSI infrastructure is building during the rest of the session.

**Impeachment Reform — Enumerated Triggers.** Defining specific enumerated triggers for impeachment makes the process more legible, more defensible, and harder to characterize as partisan weaponization. A good-government reform that strengthens institutional accountability without requiring partisan framing.

## Layer 3 — Core Democratic Infrastructure

**Timing: June–September 2029.**

These bills build the institutional infrastructure on which the rest of the stack depends. More complex and more likely to require sustained floor time, but they must be in place before subsequent layers can function.

**Campaign Finance Reform — Tiered Individual Donations and the National Democracy Fund.** The financial backbone of party formation and representation fairness. Verified individual donations, small-donor matching, the Democracy Fund Portal, and the National Democracy Fund must be operational before the party recognition framework can function. The small-donor breadth requirement is meaningless without a functioning matching infrastructure. Political case: this replaces corporate and dark-money influence with citizen-verified funding. The organized opposition is politically unpopular.

**Digital Democracy Platform.** The civic signal infrastructure underlying constituent routing, semiannual accountability sessions, mandate tracking, and the representation heatmap. Technical infrastructure, not contested policy — easy to defend publicly.

**Federal Electoral Integrity Commission — Establishment.** The institutional linchpin of the entire electoral reform layer. Certifies state electoral methods, administers the party recognition framework, reviews caucus formation filings, compiles candidate public record data, and enforces anti-entrenchment standards. Without it, the representation fairness and party formation frameworks have no enforcement mechanism.

The FEIC establishment bill must include the full anti-capture architecture: staggered seven-year nonrenewable terms, multi-body appointment process, strict conflict-of-interest restrictions, and explicit prohibition on sharing leadership with DoDA. It must be funded at a level sufficient to actually operate — an underfunded FEIC is a captured FEIC in slow motion.

**Judicial Reform Statutory Track.** Judicial ethics, disclosure, recusal rules, JNC design, and any statutory senior-status rotation bridge are sequenced in the Judicial Reform brief. They may proceed in parallel with Layer 3 because they are statutory and institutionally adjacent to democratic integrity. They are not gating dependencies for the electoral-market stack. Hard SCOTUS tenure limits remain part of the constitutional mandate layer.

## Layer 4 — NRM Stack-Integrity Activation and First Public Mandate Cycle

**Timing: Mid-2029, concurrent with late Layer 3 legislative work.**

The NRM is enacted as a complete, legally reviewed instrument. The full architecture — certification panel design, Factual Claims Panel, deliberation window requirements, spending architecture, severable title structure, sunset-on-failure mechanism — was designed and adversarially reviewed in Layer 0. What is staged is not the design but the political enactment of each architectural component. Layer 0 defines the full architecture in advance; later enactment of specific components is staged politically, not invented after obstruction.

The November 2029 ballot is achievable not because the NRM is enacted early enough to run a full certification cycle from scratch, but because the title language, public materials, GAO plain-language review, and legal hardening were prepared in Layer 0. The pre-certification work is already done. The NRM enactment in mid-2029 activates a vessel that is ready to use, not one being built on the fly.

The first NRM ballot may include titles from other domain sequencing documents, including the Culture War Settlement Stack, if those titles have completed their own legal and implementation review. In this Democratic Integrity sequencing brief, those titles are relevant only as NRM proof points. Their substantive sequencing belongs to the Culture War domain.

**Initial enactment scope.** On day one, the NRM activates with: the *Field v. Clark* contingent-legislation structure, severable title architecture, sunset-on-failure mechanism, defined implementation pathway requirement, spending disclosure through the Democracy Fund Portal, and GAO-produced plain language ballot questions. The Referendum Certification Panel, Factual Claims Panel, full public matching, and contribution limits to certified campaign committees — all fully designed in Layer 0 — are enacted as the political sequencing warrants. This is staged deployment of a pre-designed system, not improvisation.

### NRM Activation Guardrails

The NRM requires consistent minimum legitimacy safeguards from its first use: public title text, neutral plain-language ballot questions, a minimum publication window, spending disclosure through the Democracy Fund Portal, severability, sunset-on-failure, and a defined implementation pathway for each title. These are not optional in early rounds. They are the floor the full architecture is built on top of.

For early or off-year use, the NRM should be used primarily for mandate-establishment or low-risk activation titles. High-salience structural governance reforms move through the full certification architecture as it is enacted — but the design of that architecture does not wait on the political timing of its deployment.

### November 2029 Ballot

The November 2029 ballot may include two kinds of titles.

The first are cross-domain settlement titles that have completed their own domain sequencing and legal review. Culture-war settlement titles are likely candidates if their domain sequencing is ready, but they are not Democratic Integrity titles.

The second are Democratic Integrity mandate titles — especially constitutional-mandate questions for term limits, age limits, Electoral College reform, or SCOTUS active-service limits. These questions do not enact constitutional change. They generate a public mandate and trigger the mandate-implementation machinery.

The strategic logic: a November 2029 ratification result showing 65–70%+ support for term limits creates maximum pressure on the 2030 midterm cycle. Members who vote against implementing the mandate must defend that vote against a documented public mandate in the next election cycle.

### January 2030 — Constitutional Mandate Implementation Act

The January 2030 legislation does not impose congressional term or age limits directly. Those reforms require constitutional amendment. Instead, the Act operationalizes the public mandate created by the NRM vote.

It adopts congressional findings recognizing the ratification result; publishes model Article V application language for state legislatures; establishes state-by-state and district-by-district mandate reports; requires Candidate Nutrition Label disclosure of each member's position on implementing the mandate; authorizes voluntary party and caucus pledge rules; creates a public tracker for state Article V applications; and formally recommends that states submit identically scoped Article V applications.

The political function is direct: the public creates the mandate, and incumbents must either transmit that mandate into the Article V process or block it in daylight before the next midterm cycle.

### Full NRM Certification Architecture — Staged Political Deployment

The Referendum Certification Panel, Factual Claims Panel, full public matching, contribution limits to certified committees, and the 180-day deliberation window as a hard structural requirement are fully designed and legally reviewed in Layer 0. Their political enactment is staged based on when the mandate and obstruction dynamics make deployment most effective.

If the November 2029 results produce strong support and the mandate is acted on without significant obstruction, the political case for deploying the full panel architecture may not yet be urgent. If Congress delays or functionally negates a ratified mandate, the political case for activating the Certification Panel and Factual Claims Panel makes itself — not because the design is being invented in response to obstruction, but because the pre-designed architecture is now being deployed at the moment it carries the most political weight.

The same logic applies to Tier 3 thresholds. The 60% geographic supermajority standard is designed in full during Layer 0. It is deployed when the constitutional mandate ask requires that level of legitimacy weight. Build the threshold to fit the task — but build it before the task arrives, not during it.

## Layer 5 — Electoral Market Opening

**Timing: March–October 2030.**

With infrastructure operational and the first NRM results in hand, the electoral reform layer is enacted ahead of the November 2030 midterms. The political environment has shifted: the majority has a demonstrated public mandate from the 2029 ratification results, and incumbents who obstruct are doing so in a midterm year where the reform coalition is actively campaigning against them.

**Representation Fairness and Electoral Method Choice.** Establishes the federal democratic integrity floor, the certified state electoral method menu, the anti-entrenchment architecture, and the representation heatmap infrastructure. The anti-entrenchment certification mandate is enacted by ordinary statute under Congress's Article I federal election authority — not NRM-contingent. The state adoption incentive package goes through the NRM as a Tier 1 or Tier 2 title on the November 2030 ballot.

**State Party Formation and Recognition.** Establishes the party recognition framework only — membership breadth, small-donor breadth, transparent governance, financial disclosure, anti-collusion compliance. Congressional caucus rights are not bundled here. See Layer 6.

**Candidate Nutrition Label and Mandate Accountability Infrastructure.** Four-tier candidate schema, mandate contribution tracking, DoDA certification architecture, semiannual accountability loop.

**Election Day and Primary Reform — Unified Primary Calendar.** Unified national Primary Day one year before the general election. Must be enacted before the 2030 cycle to govern the 2031 primary calendar that feeds into 2032.

### November 2030 — Second NRM Ballot and Early-Adopter Electoral-Method Pilots

November 2030 should be treated as an early-adopter and conditional pilot cycle, not a guaranteed nationwide transition. NRM titles cover the state adoption incentive package and representation heatmap infrastructure. States that completed Layer 0 readiness work may run certified proportional or semi-proportional methods. Broader adoption likely develops over the 2032 and 2034 cycles. New party candidates compete where recognition infrastructure is active.

## Layer 6 — Coalition Architecture and NRM Maturation

**Timing: 2031–2032, first session after the 2030 election.**

This layer activates once the first election cycle under the new system has run. Its political conditions are fundamentally different from earlier layers: the reform coalition now includes elected officials who won under the new system with direct institutional incentives to defend and extend it.

**Congressional Caucus Bloc Rights.** Introduced in the new session with new members present. Establishes formal institutional rights for recognized congressional caucus blocs: committee assignment proportionality, staff allocation, leadership negotiation standing, floor procedural rights. The floor debate features members of new parties explaining in their own voices why the existing structure does not accommodate the constituencies that elected them — a categorically different political dynamic from any earlier version of this bill.

**Full NRM Certification Architecture — Staged Deployment.** The full panel architecture was designed in Layer 0. If the earlier NRM rounds have produced clean implementation, the political conditions for deploying the Certification Panel and Factual Claims Panel as enacted components may not yet be urgent. If Congress has attempted to delay or functionally negate ratified mandates, those pre-designed components are deployed at the moment they carry maximum political weight — arriving with a clear public justification rather than as a bureaucratic pre-commitment. The full architecture is also eligible to be ratified as a Tier 1 or Tier 2 NRM title, giving the machinery itself a public mandate that makes it harder for a future administration to dismantle.

**Tier 2 NRM Ratification Cycle.** Structural governance reform titles go to ratification on the next qualifying Election Day. Tier 2 requires 55% support on the ratification question, placement on an election meeting the Tier 2 participation floor, and passage in states representing at least 50% of the national eligible voting population.

**Article V Convention Application Campaign.** With the 2029 mandate result and 2030 NRM results documented, the convention application campaign intensifies in non-majority-controlled states. The campaign targets the remaining applications needed to reach 34.

**Presidential Caucus Bloc Formation Begins.** Congressional caucus blocs operating through the 2031–2032 session provide the evidentiary foundation for presidential caucus bloc formation. The 2032 presidential election is likely a transitional cycle — the full presidential architecture is more realistically a 2036 target after two full congressional cycles of caucus alignment have accumulated.

## Layer 7 — Constitutional Mandate Layer

**Timing: 2032 and beyond.**

### Mechanism 1 — Pre-Scoped Article V Convention

Article V provides two routes to proposing amendments: two-thirds of both chambers, or a convention called by two-thirds of state legislatures (34 states). The convention route has never been used but is constitutionally available. Its state legislature math may be more favorable than the congressional math — particularly for term limits, where the opposition includes members of the majority's own caucus who would be personally affected.

All 34 state applications must be specifically and identically scoped — to "congressional renewal, term limits, and age limits for federal elected officials" — rather than to general constitutional revision. Identical narrow scoping minimizes the aggregation ambiguity in Article V's "same subject" requirement and maximizes the legal defense against runaway convention claims. The ultimate anti-runaway safeguard is that any amendment proposed by a convention still requires ratification by three-fourths of state legislatures — 38 states must independently approve it.

A successful Tier 3 NRM ratification — 60% support on the ratification question, placement on an election meeting the Tier 3 participation floor, and passage in states representing at least 60% of the national eligible voting population — creates direct political accountability pressure on state legislators in non-majority-controlled states. The NRM result does not legally compel state legislatures. It makes inaction politically costly in a way that is legible to voters and trackable through the candidate nutrition label infrastructure.

Delegate selection and scope enforcement require a separate Article V implementation brief. Options include state legislative appointment, public election, or hybrid certification models. This sequencing brief does not resolve that design choice; it identifies it as a critical dependency.

**Electoral College restructuring.** The National Popular Vote Interstate Compact provides a parallel pathway — it activates when states representing 270 electoral votes have joined and requires no federal legislation. The Compact and the constitutional amendment track are complementary, not competing.

### Mechanism 2 — Statutory Senior-Status Rotation for SCOTUS

Sequenced in the Judicial Reform brief and enacted in parallel with Layer 3. The constitutional fix — hard SCOTUS tenure limits — is part of this layer if Article V application language is drafted broadly enough to cover judicial active-service limits alongside legislative term limits. That is a separate design question for the Article V implementation brief.

## Legal Pressure Escalation Doctrine

Legal challenges to NRM titles and implementing legislation are expected. They are not automatically failure states. When a public mandate is clear, the fallback path is prepared, and the opposition is forced to own obstruction in public, litigation can become a pressure multiplier. The mechanism should be designed to survive most challenges, fall back cleanly when it does not, and convert obstruction into visible democratic accountability. Legal defeat without a prepared fallback is still defeat; the pressure doctrine only works when the next lawful step is ready.

**Challenge to the lean NRM mechanism.** A court strikes down the *Field v. Clark* argument as applied to a national public ratification vote. Congress re-enacts the mandate as ordinary statute under the documented public mandate. Members who vote against implementation must defend that vote against a documented public mandate in the next election cycle.

**Strike-down of ordinary statute.** A court invalidates the statutory implementation of a ratified mandate on substantive grounds. This is when the Tier 3 constitutional mandate track becomes urgent. A judiciary blocking a strong popular mandate provides the clearest possible argument for constitutional amendment. The Article V campaign now has a concrete institutional conflict to organize around.

**Challenge to the full NRM certification architecture.** If spending cap provisions are struck on First Amendment grounds, the foreign-money ban, disclosure requirements, public matching, and contribution limits to certified committees remain operative by severability. The mechanism continues.

**Challenge to the Article V convention itself.** A court attempts to enjoin or invalidate the convention. The opposition must now own the position that they are using the judiciary to block a constitutional process the Founders explicitly wrote as an alternative to congressional self-interest. This institutional conflict may also make a direct congressional two-thirds vote more viable than it was before — once the opposition has publicly aligned itself with judicial blockade of a demonstrated mandate, some members of the opposition caucus face their own electoral accountability for that position. That outcome is not guaranteed, but the conditions for it are better than they were before the challenge.

## Dependency Map

**Gating/core:** FEIC establishment, SSI/voter registration, campaign finance rails, Election Day reform — must be operational before the electoral market layer functions.

**Electoral market layer:** Representation fairness (anti-entrenchment by statute, incentive package via NRM), party formation and recognition, candidate nutrition label — builds the competitive political market.

**Coalition layer:** Congressional caucus bloc rights (Layer 6, after new members arrive), presidential caucus bloc architecture — activates as new parties win seats.

**Legitimacy layer:** NRM — lean version enacted in Layer 4, matures through demonstrated use, Tier 2 and Tier 3 architecture built when obstruction warrants it.

**Constitutional layer:** Legislative term limits, age limits, Electoral College restructuring, SCOTUS hard tenure limits — Article V convention and NRM Tier 3 mandate, 2033+ horizon.

**Cross-domain interfaces:** Technology/Data, Culture War, Judicial Reform, and other domain stacks may share tools or mandate infrastructure with Democratic Integrity, but their substantive sequencing belongs to their own domain briefs.

## Pressure Dynamics on Non-Adopting States

Three mechanisms create pressure on states that resist certified proportional methods without federal compulsion.

**Congressional representation divergence.** Early-adopter states produce proportional delegations whose members represent a wider range of political formations. The contrast with winner-take-all states becomes visible and politically salient over time.

**Political market pressure.** Voters in non-adopting states observe proportional representation producing genuine competition in neighboring states and generate pressure on their own state legislatures. Demonstrated performance in comparable contexts changes what voters believe is possible.

**FEIC certification consequences.** States that fail FEIC certification face federal election administration funding consequences and public reporting of failures. Repeated proportionality failures create a documented public record that becomes a political liability for state legislators defending the status quo.

## Failure Mode Sequencing

Each layer is independently valuable. The constitutional layer is the ceiling, not the floor.

**If the filibuster reform fails.** Some Layer 2 elements may attach to must-pass legislation. The election day holiday and stock trading ban are likely candidates for reconciliation attachment. The rest requires direct floor votes.

**If the FEIC establishment is blocked or captured.** The representation fairness and party formation frameworks lose their enforcement mechanism. This is the highest-priority single point of failure. The FEIC must be funded at a level that makes it operationally independent — an FEIC dependent on annual appropriations is vulnerable to defunding as de facto capture.

**If early NRM ratification cycles fail.** Failed titles sunset and expire. The reform coalition must assess whether the question was poorly framed, deliberation was insufficient, or public support was narrower than polling suggested — and adjust before resubmission.

**If state proportional adoption stalls.** Congressional caucus diversity develops more slowly. The presidential architecture's proving-ground stage is delayed. The full presidential system becomes operational later. This is the most likely form of partial success.

**If legal challenges succeed at multiple levels.** Apply the legal pressure escalation doctrine — but only when the next lawful step is prepared. Re-enact under demonstrated mandate. Advance the constitutional track. Each challenge the opposition wins publicly adds to the obstruction record, but the doctrine only converts pressure into outcomes when fallback architecture is ready in advance.
