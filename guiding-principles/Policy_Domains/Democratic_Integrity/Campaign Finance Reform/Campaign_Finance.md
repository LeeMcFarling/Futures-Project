# Campaign Finance Reform: Tiered Individual Donations & the National Democracy Fund

## Summary
This proposal creates a transparent, citizen-led campaign funding system. All campaign contributions must come from **verified individuals**, eliminating corporate, PAC, and dark-money influence.  
Small donations are **publicly matched** to amplify grassroots voices, while large individual donations remain capped and fully transparent.

---

## Rationale
Current campaign finance laws let corporations and billionaires dominate the political process through super PACs and dark-money networks.  
Ordinary voters are priced out of influence.  
By limiting donations to individuals — and amplifying small contributions with public funds — we restore *equality of voice* in democracy.

---

## Proposal

### 1. Who Can Donate
- Only **natural persons** who are U.S. citizens.  
- No corporations, unions, foreign nationals, PACs, or nonprofit intermediaries may donate or spend on behalf of campaigns.

---

### 2. Tiered Donation Limits
| Tier | Limit | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Small Donor** | Up to $200 per election | Eligible for 5:1 public matching through the National Democracy Fund. |
| **Large Donor** | Up to $5,000 per election | Allowed but must be publicly disclosed in real time. No matching. |
| **Aggregate Cap** | $10,000 per cycle per donor | Across all federal races combined. |

All donations are processed through the **Democracy Fund Portal**, ensuring instant public disclosure.

---

### 3. The National Democracy Fund
- Financed by:
  - A **1% VAT** on luxury goods and services.  
  - A **0.1% financial transaction tax** on speculative trades.  
- Administered by an independent **Federal Democracy Commission**.  
- Provides 5:1 matching on verified small donations.  
- Publishes all transactions to a **public blockchain ledger** in real time.

---

### 4. Enforcement & Transparency
- All campaigns must use approved **Democracy Fund accounts**.  
- The **FEC** (or successor commission) enforces contribution caps, audits accounts, and prosecutes violations.  
- Third-party spending is prohibited unless donors are fully disclosed.  
- Anonymous donations above $50 are illegal.

---

### 5. Global Precedents
- **Canada**: limits individual donations to citizens; corporations and unions banned.  
- **Germany**: public matching system for small donors.  
- **France**: public funding plus donation caps ensure parity.  

*Lesson:* healthy democracies rely on public trust, not private capital.

---

## Fiscal Impact
A 1% VAT and 0.1% transaction tax would generate **$3–5 billion annually**, funding small-donor matches and debate access programs while fully replacing corporate influence in elections.

## Open Questions
