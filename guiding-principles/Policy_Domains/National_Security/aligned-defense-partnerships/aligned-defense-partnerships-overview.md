## Purpose

Aligned Defense Partnerships (ADPs) define a category of security relationships through which the United States and partner states maintain cooperation, interoperability, and strategic alignment without establishing formal security backstop arrangements or regional coordination memberships.

This framework recognizes that many countries share overlapping security interests, maintain bilateral defense cooperation agreements, or participate in joint exercises and capability development initiatives without requiring participation in deeper institutional security architectures such as Integrated Defense Partnerships or Security Backstop Partnerships.

---

## Alignment Without Institutional Membership

ADPs provide structured pathways for cooperation based on common interests rather than formalized regional security governance.

Partner states may engage in:

- bilateral defense consultation  
- training and interoperability initiatives  
- intelligence or technical cooperation  
- defense procurement relationships  
- participation in multinational exercises  

These relationships remain flexible and evolve according to political priorities and strategic conditions.

---

## Relationship to Regional Security Architectures

ADP states may interface with regional security bodies established under Security Backstop Partnerships or integrated defense partners (IDPs) where interests overlap.

Such engagement may include:

- participation in specific coordination initiatives  
- observer or dialogue roles within regional forums  
- cooperation on defined stabilization efforts  
- coordination of support during crises  

Involvement in regional activities does not constitute membership in SBP governance structures.

---

## External Support Pathways

Aligned partners may request support through existing bilateral channels or participate in regionally coordinated stabilization efforts when appropriate.

Where regional security councils determine that broader participation would enhance collective security, ADP states may be invited to contribute capabilities, expertise, or diplomatic support.

Support decisions remain sovereign and context-specific.

---

## Sovereignty and Flexibility

ADPs do not create mutual defense obligations, institutional decision-making commitments, or long-term governance integration requirements.

This flexibility allows states to maintain strategic autonomy while benefiting from predictable cooperation frameworks.

---

## Strategic Effect

Aligned Defense Partnerships expand the network of cooperative security relationships without requiring uniform institutional depth.

This layered approach enables the United States and partner states to maintain strategic coordination across diverse geopolitical environments while preserving options for future engagement within more structured architectures if conditions evolve.
