# Gulf Integrated Defense Compact (GIDC)

## Purpose

The Gulf Integrated Defense Compact establishes a regional defensive coordination framework designed to protect civilian populations, economic infrastructure, and maritime commerce in the Gulf region.

The compact focuses on two primary missions:

- missile and aerial defense of regional infrastructure  
- protection of commercial shipping and economic activity

The compact is a defensive coordination architecture intended to improve early warning, crisis coordination, and infrastructure protection. The framework does not constitute an offensive military alliance and does not impose collective military obligations on participating states.

All military decisions remain under sovereign national authority.

---

# Strategic Context

Recent conflicts in the region have demonstrated the vulnerability of:

- energy infrastructure  
- civilian population centers  
- commercial shipping routes  
- port and desalination facilities  

Missile attacks, drone strikes, and maritime disruptions pose systemic risks not only to Gulf states but to global energy markets and international trade.

The Gulf Integrated Defense Compact (GIDC) establishes a standing regional coordination framework designed to protect the economic and civilian systems of Gulf nations. The framework can be activated during periods of crisis to coordinate missile defense, infrastructure protection, and maritime security, while also serving as a structured coordination platform through which the United States and other security partners can support regional defensive operations when necessary.

---

# Phase I Architecture

Phase I establishes a regional coordination structure focused on defensive awareness and crisis coordination.

The framework consists of three primary components:

1. Integrated Missile and Aerial Defense Network  
2. Maritime and Commercial Security Coordination  
3. Regional Air and Missile Coordination Center (RAMCC)

---

# Integrated Missile and Aerial Defense Network

## Objective

Participating states will establish a shared defensive awareness network designed to detect, track, and coordinate responses to missile, drone, and aerial threats across the Gulf region. 

The system integrates national sensor systems into a shared regional early-warning architecture.

## Shared Early Warning System

The network integrates data from:

- radar systems
- air defense sensors
- missile launch detection systems
- satellite warning systems
- maritime surveillance platforms

The system provides a shared regional operating picture tracking:

- ballistic missile launches
- cruise missile activity
- drone threats
- military aircraft movement

Participating states retain full authority over national defensive responses.

---

# Maritime and Commercial Security Coordination

## Objective

Protect commercial shipping lanes and economic infrastructure in the Gulf and Strait of Hormuz from missile, drone, and maritime threats.

## Core Functions

Participating states coordinate:

- maritime surveillance of key shipping corridors
- defensive naval patrol awareness
- threat alerts to commercial vessels
- infrastructure protection planning

Priority protection areas include:

- oil and gas facilities
- major ports
- desalination plants
- international shipping routes
- energy export infrastructure

The goal is to preserve uninterrupted commercial activity and global energy supply stability.

---

# Regional Air and Missile Coordination Center (RAMCC)

## Role

The Regional Air and Missile Coordination Center serves as the operational coordination hub of the Gulf Integrated Defense Compact.

The center maintains a shared regional air and maritime defense picture during peacetime and facilitates defensive coordination among participating states during periods of regional crisis.

The coordination center enables participating states to share early warning information, maintain situational awareness of aerial and missile threats, and coordinate defensive actions to protect civilian populations, infrastructure, and commercial activity.

The RAMCC does not exercise command authority over national forces.

All operational decisions remain under sovereign national control.

The center provides a coordination platform through which participating states, and supporting security partners when requested, can align defensive activities and share threat awareness during periods of heightened regional risk.

---

## Core Functions

The RAMCC supports participating states through:

- real-time threat monitoring
- shared missile launch detection alerts
- airspace coordination
- defensive aircraft awareness
- maritime security coordination
- infrastructure protection alerts

---

# Crisis Activation Protocol

The GIDC shifts from monitoring posture to coordinated defensive posture when one or more of the following conditions occur:

- missile or drone attacks within the Gulf region
- credible intelligence of imminent aerial attack
- attacks on energy or civilian infrastructure
- disruption or threat to maritime commerce
- request for activation by a participating member state

Upon activation the RAMCC establishes continuous coordination channels between defense authorities.

---

# Crisis Coordination Measures

When activated, the compact supports:

- synchronized regional early warning alerts
- airspace coordination between defensive aircraft
- interceptor engagement awareness
- maritime patrol coordination
- infrastructure defense alerts

These measures aim to improve defensive response times while reducing the risk of operational miscalculation.

---

# Operational Principles

The Gulf Integrated Defense Compact operates under four principles.

## Defensive Mission

The compact exists solely to protect civilian populations, economic infrastructure, and maritime commerce.

## Sovereign Authority

Each participating state retains full control over military decisions.

## Economic Stability

Protection of global energy flows and maritime commerce is a core mission.

## Crisis Coordination

The compact provides a standing framework for regional coordination during periods of conflict or instability.

---

# Phase I Deliverables

Initial implementation focuses on:

- establishing the RAMCC coordination center
- integrating shared early warning networks
- developing maritime security coordination protocols
- establishing crisis activation procedures

Future phases may expand interoperability, joint exercises, and regional defense cooperation.
