# Venezuela Resource Trust (VRT)

## Purpose

The Venezuela Resource Trust (VRT) establishes a **sovereign-owned, externally custodied, rule-locked financial system** for managing oil revenues in a manner that:

- prevents political capture and confiscation  
- restores international investment credibility  
- stabilizes fiscal flows across economic cycles  
- converts natural resource wealth into long-term national assets  

The system is designed to function under **high political volatility**, ensuring continuity across administrations.

---

## Core Design Principle

**Sovereignty is preserved through rules, not discretion.**

The Venezuelan state retains full ownership of national resources and associated revenues.

However, **access to those revenues is governed by pre-committed, non-discretionary rules** enforced through legal, financial, and technical constraints.

---

## I. Structural Architecture

### 1. Sovereign Ownership

- All assets are legally owned by the Venezuelan state  
- The Trust operates as a **sovereign financial vehicle**, not a private fund  
- Revenues originate from oil production, royalties, and associated contracts  

---

### 2. External Custody Layer

Assets are held in **multi-jurisdictional custody structures** across:

- United Kingdom  
- United States  
- Norway  

Custody design ensures:

- no single jurisdiction can unilaterally release funds  
- domestic political actors cannot directly access principal  
- assets remain insulated from domestic legal seizure  

---

### 3. Governance Firewall

The Trust operates under a **three-layer governance model**:

#### A. Rule Engine (Primary Authority)

- Deposits, allocations, and withdrawals are governed by **codified formulas**
- No executive, legislative, or ministerial override authority exists

#### B. Independent Technical Manager

- Global asset managers execute investment strategy  
- Mandate: maximize long-term, risk-adjusted returns  
- No authority over allocation or withdrawal decisions  

#### C. Oversight Council

- Venezuelan representatives (multi-party)  
- Civil society and diaspora representation  
- Neutral international observers (e.g., Norwegian technical participation)

Role:
- audit compliance  
- publish transparency reports  
- no discretionary control over funds  

---

## II. Revenue Flow Mechanism

### Incremental Production Allocation Model

The VRT applies initially to:

> **All new or incremental oil production generated post-implementation**

Revenue allocation:

- X% → Venezuelan fiscal budget (immediate use)  
- Y% → Venezuela Resource Trust (locked principal)  

This avoids:

- fiscal shock  
- political rejection  
- disruption of existing obligations  

---

## III. Fiscal Rule System

### 1. Principal Protection

- The Trust principal is **non-withdrawable under all standard conditions**

---

### 2. Return-Based Spending Rule

- Annual government access limited to:
  
  > **Expected real return (3–4%) of total assets**

- Distributed automatically via a **budget smoothing mechanism**

---

### 3. Automatic Allocation

All flows are:

- formula-driven  
- time-scheduled  
- publicly auditable  

No discretionary budgeting interface exists within the Trust.

---

## IV. Legal Constraint Framework

### 1. Constitutional Embedding

The fiscal rule and non-withdrawal constraint are:

- codified at the constitutional level  
- binding across administrations  

---

### 2. International Legal Anchoring

Custody and enforcement rely on:

- international financial law  
- multi-jurisdictional contractual obligations  

Violation triggers:

- automatic suspension of access flows  
- legal enforcement actions  
- reputational penalties in capital markets  

---

## V. Transparency and Accountability

### Department of Data and Accountability (DoDa) Integration

- Real-time public dashboards tracking:
  - inflows  
  - asset allocation  
  - returns  
  - distributions  

- Independent audit cycles  

- Open data access for:
  - citizens  
  - investors  
  - international institutions  

---

## VI. Political Transition Compatibility

The VRT is designed to:

- function under current conditions  
- scale under a democratic transition  
- persist across future administrations  

### Activation Pathway

Full system activation may be tied to:

- internationally recognized elections  
- sanctions relief triggers  
- verified production increases  

---

## VII. Strategic Effects

### Economic

- restores investor confidence  
- enables large-scale capital inflows  
- stabilizes currency and fiscal expectations  

---

### Political

- reduces incentive for resource-driven corruption  
- limits executive overreach  
- creates durable cross-party constraint  

---

### Geopolitical

- reduces external coercion leverage  
- reintegrates Venezuela into global energy markets  
- stabilizes regional migration and economic flows  

---

## VIII. Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: Sovereignty Narrative Backlash

Mitigation:
- explicit sovereign ownership  
- domestic constitutional ratification  
- visible citizen benefit flows  

---

### Risk: Political Attempts to Override

Mitigation:
- external custody  
- multi-jurisdiction enforcement  
- automatic flow suspension  

---

### Risk: Parallel Shadow Revenue Systems

Mitigation:
- tie new production licenses to VRT participation  
- enforce compliance through export channels  

---

## IX. Bottom Line

The Venezuela Resource Trust does not rely on trust in political actors.

It replaces trust with:

- **rules that cannot be overridden**  
- **custody that cannot be accessed unilaterally**  
- **transparency that cannot be obscured**  

The objective is simple:

> Ensure that Venezuela’s resource wealth benefits its citizens over decades—  
not its political cycles.
