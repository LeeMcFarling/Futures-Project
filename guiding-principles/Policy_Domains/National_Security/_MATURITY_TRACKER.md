## Domain Overview

**Purpose:**
Define America's security posture, alliance structures, and strategic approach to adversaries and global challenges.

**Key Questions:**
- What is our strategic posture toward China and Russia?
- How do we maintain alliance commitments while burden-sharing?
- What role does cyber/space/AI play in future defense?

**Success Criteria:**
- Clear strategic framework for great power competition
- Alliance modernization with defined commitments
- Integrated cyber/space defense strategy

## Notes & Context

- Domain has **37 substantive files** — IDP and Procurement at Phase 3; China Strategy now Phase 3; domain advanced to Phase 3 overall
- **Gray-zone automaticity** is the most operationally distinctive feature of this platform's security architecture — response curves negotiated per theater in advance, tied to observable effects rather than intent attribution; proxy war cost linkage doubles adversary's cost on second infraction, becomes standing support on third; removes ad hoc deliberation that adversaries exploit
- **GDIC Phase III Political Conditioning** is the most sophisticated Middle East framework from any current democratic initiative: US + 8 Gulf states co-govern consequences; Israel, Palestine, and Iran each face phase-gated access/isolation simultaneously; proxy linkage rule closes Iran's deniability gap; nuclear governance principle tied to phase gates
- **Low-trust engagement doctrine** (Day 0 baseline rule) structurally resolves the election cycle manipulation problem: adversaries cannot time concessions to influence domestic elections because Day 0 baseline is set by behavior at engagement start, not at time of deal; multi-track phase architecture allows domain-specific progress without full bilateral normalization
- **SBP design** avoids NATO-style mutual defense treaty entanglement while still creating regional coordination infrastructure; RSC model distributes governance without US being the hub of every spoke
- **UN Reform** approach avoids both US withdrawal (ceding multilateral legitimacy) and ineffective reform rhetoric: RSCs as distributed legitimacy mechanisms; conflict-of-interest screening as institutional safeguard; enforcement ladder as operational capability
- Three-tier architecture: IDP (deep integration) → SBP (regional backstop councils) → ADP (bilateral cooperation) — each tier has distinct integration and governance requirements; not a single model applied uniformly
- IDP: 12 files; Procurement_Reform: 3 files (Phase 3); Gulf_Defense_Compact: 3 files (Phase I → II → III complete)
- Foreign policy: 4 files — internal stability as FP objective #1 (reorientation from democracy-promotion to resilience); low-trust engagement doctrine at Phase 2–3; legal compliance restoration; Venezuela case study
- **China Strategy** (v0.3) closes the domain's most significant gap — managed competition operating system: phase-gated tracks, Day 0 baseline rule, gray-zone aggregation, Taiwan stability, legitimacy repair, strategic non-goals; physically in foreign-policy/ folder, subdomain China_Strategy
- **Cyber Security** covered cross-domain: `Technology_and_Data/Technology/cybersecurity-architecture-and-identity-modernization.md` (v0.2) — zero-trust architecture, SSI/UPI identity layer, IDP digital infrastructure linkage; a NS-specific deterrence/offensive doctrine companion may be warranted at Phase 4 but is not a blocking gap
- **No remaining Phase 0 gaps** in the domain
- Indo-Pacific: SBP and ADP architecture provides the framework; regional instantiation not yet written
