## Domain Overview

**Purpose:**
Define America's security posture, alliance structures, and strategic approach to adversaries and global challenges through a three-tier partnership architecture — Integrated Defense Partnerships (IDP, deep integration), Security Backstop Partnerships (SBP, regional non-alliance coordination councils), and Aligned Defense Partnerships (ADP, bilateral cooperation) — with pre-specified gray-zone automaticity, phase-gated regional political conditioning through the Gulf Integrated Defense Compact (GDIC), low-trust engagement doctrine, and a UN reform stack that distributes multilateral legitimacy without great-power veto paralysis.

**Key Questions:**
- How do we maintain alliance commitments while reducing single-point-of-failure dependency on US commitment?
- What industrial and supply chain architecture (N+2 redundancy, Arsenal bifurcation, Alliance Offtake Compact) supports sustained deterrence without industrial collapse risk?
- How do we manage gray-zone conflict without ad hoc crisis deliberation — and without creating escalation ladders adversaries can exploit?
- How do we engage low-trust adversaries without either freezing cooperation or being manipulated through election-cycle incentives?

**Success Criteria:**
- Three-tier partnership taxonomy operational (IDP/SBP/ADP), each with distinct integration and governance requirements
- Arsenal bifurcation: Tier A (IDP-standard consumable systems, N+2 multi-node production) and Tier B (sovereign crown-jewel systems) architecturally separated
- Gray-zone response curves pre-negotiated per theater, tied to observable effects rather than intent attribution
- GDIC Phase III Political Conditioning Framework (PCF) operational: phase-gated access for Israel, Palestine, and Iran simultaneously; US + 8 Gulf states co-govern consequences
- SBP regional councils deployed in at least one pilot region, RSC governance architecture operational
- Low-trust engagement doctrine (Day 0 baseline rule, multi-track phase architecture) codified as executive doctrine
- UN Security Council reform path with Regional Security Councils as legitimacy distribution mechanism; conflict-of-interest screening operational

## Notes & Context

- Domain has **36 substantive files** — significantly more developed than the Phase 2 overall rating suggests; IDP and Procurement are at Phase 3, most subdomains at Phase 2–3
- **Gray-zone automaticity** is the most operationally distinctive feature of this platform's security architecture — response curves negotiated per theater in advance, tied to observable effects rather than intent attribution; proxy war cost linkage doubles adversary's cost on second infraction, becomes standing support on third; removes ad hoc deliberation that adversaries exploit
- **GDIC Phase III Political Conditioning** is the most sophisticated Middle East framework from any current democratic initiative: US + 8 Gulf states co-govern consequences; Israel, Palestine, and Iran each face phase-gated access/isolation simultaneously; proxy linkage rule closes Iran's deniability gap; nuclear governance principle tied to phase gates
- **Low-trust engagement doctrine** (Day 0 baseline rule) structurally resolves the election cycle manipulation problem: adversaries cannot time concessions to influence domestic elections because Day 0 baseline is set by behavior at engagement start, not at time of deal; multi-track phase architecture allows domain-specific progress without full bilateral normalization
- **SBP design** avoids NATO-style mutual defense treaty entanglement while still creating regional coordination infrastructure; RSC model distributes governance without US being the hub of every spoke
- **UN Reform** approach avoids both US withdrawal (ceding multilateral legitimacy) and ineffective reform rhetoric: RSCs as distributed legitimacy mechanisms; conflict-of-interest screening as institutional safeguard; enforcement ladder as operational capability
- Three-tier architecture: IDP (deep integration) → SBP (regional backstop councils) → ADP (bilateral cooperation) — each tier has distinct integration and governance requirements; not a single model applied uniformly
- IDP: 12 files; Procurement_Reform: 3 files (Phase 3); Gulf_Defense_Compact: 3 files (Phase I → II → III complete)
- Foreign policy: 4 files — internal stability as FP objective #1 (reorientation from democracy-promotion to resilience); low-trust engagement doctrine at Phase 2–3; legal compliance restoration; Venezuela case study
- Genuine Phase 0 gaps: China Strategy (most important missing piece — no comprehensive competitive framework) and Cyber Security national strategy (distinct from Technology_and_Data cybersecurity architecture — strategic deterrence dimension missing)
- Indo-Pacific: SBP and ADP architecture provides the framework; regional instantiation not yet written
