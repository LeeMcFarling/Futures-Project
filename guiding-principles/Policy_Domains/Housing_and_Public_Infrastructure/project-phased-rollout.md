# Phased Re-Urbanization Rollout

## Purpose

This document defines the **sequencing and gating logic** for re-urbanization projects delivered through Special Transit Zones (STZs) and the public infrastructure spine. Its purpose is to ensure that density, transit, and utilities are delivered **in the correct order**, reducing political risk, construction disruption, and speculative failure.

Re-urbanization under this framework is **phased, testable, and reversible**. Authority is granted before construction, infrastructure precedes density, and scaling occurs only after demonstrated success.

---

## Core Principle: Authority First, Density Last

Re-urbanization succeeds when **institutional capacity precedes land-use change**. This framework explicitly rejects speculative upzoning, infrastructure promises without delivery, and one-shot megaprojects.

The governing rule is:

> **Authorize → Evaluate → Build Infrastructure → Deliver Density → Scale**

---

## Phase 0 — Opt-In Pilot Authorization (Whole-Package Adoption)

States or local jurisdictions opt in to the **entire re-urbanization framework** as a bundled package. Opt-in is voluntary and binary.

At this stage:
- No land is rezoned  
- No construction is authorized  
- No development rights are granted  

Opt-in grants access to STZ authority, spine standards, pattern books, and permitting pathways. This mirrors regulatory pilot models used in other policy domains.

---

## Phase 1 — Candidate Site Designation

Within opt-in jurisdictions, sites are designated as **candidates**, not approvals.

Eligible candidates are limited to predefined node typologies:
- Big-box retail and power centers  
- Dead strip malls and declining commercial corridors  
- Stadium and event parking fields  
- Oversized arterial and highway-adjacent parcels  
- Industrial transition zones 
- Large surface parking lots already adjacent to transit nodes.  

Candidate designation confers **eligibility for evaluation only**.

---

## Phase 2 — Technical & Urban Evaluation

A multidisciplinary committee evaluates each candidate site against **objective, published criteria**, including:
- Capacity for mid-rise density  
- Alignment with the infrastructure spine  
- Utility relocation and capacity constraints  
- Parking replacement feasibility  
- Adjacency impacts and transition buffering  
- Phasing and constructability  

The committee performs **technical validation**, not discretionary design review. Sites may be approved, deferred, or rejected.

Only approved sites advance.

---

## Phase 3 — Infrastructure-First Deployment

Approved sites proceed under a **strict infrastructure-first sequence**.

### Phase 3A — Parking Consolidation

- Surface parking is replaced with structured parking  
- Existing retail, event, and neighborhood access is preserved  
- Temporary disruption is minimized  

This phase stabilizes existing uses and reduces resistance before density is introduced.

---

### Phase 3B — Public Infrastructure Spine Delivery

- Spine segments are constructed  
- Utilities and conduit superhighway are installed  
- Colonnades and public realm are delivered early  
- Transit service is activated or staged  

At completion, the site has **mobility, utilities, and civic space** in place.

---

## Phase 4 — Phased Density & Retail Build-Out

Only after infrastructure delivery do **by-right density entitlements activate**.

Development proceeds incrementally:
- Mid-rise mixed-use buildings  
- Retail and services tied to foot traffic  
- Civic and community amenities  

Large single-phase builds are generally discouraged in favor of staged infill.

---

## Phase 5 — Performance Review & Scaling

Completed phases are evaluated against **published success metrics**, including:
- Schedule and cost adherence  
- Transit usage and mode shift  
- Retail occupancy and activity  
- Public realm utilization and safety
- Tax revenue per square mile  

Successful pilots unlock:
- Additional site approvals  
- Accelerated review timelines  
- Eligibility for state or national STZ expansion  

Failure does not propagate system-wide.

---

## Why This Works

This phased approach:
- Prevents premature upzoning  
- Ensures infrastructure delivery precedes density  
- Preserves access and parking early  
- Mirrors proven pilot-based regulatory models  
- Allows rollback without systemic disruption  

Re-urbanization becomes **procedural, predictable, and scalable**.

---

## Relationship to Other Framework Components

This rollout framework governs execution across:
- Special Transit Zones (STZs)  
- Node Typologies & Re-Urbanization Triggers  
- Public Infrastructure Spine deployment  
- Regional Architectural Pattern Books  

It is the **operational backbone** of the re-urbanization system.
