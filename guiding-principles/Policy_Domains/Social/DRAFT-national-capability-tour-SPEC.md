# Civic Execution Coordination Units (CECU)

## Operational Problem

Major public initiatives in housing, healthcare, manufacturing, and infrastructure frequently fail not due to lack of funding or policy authorization, but due to insufficient coordination labor.

Projects require:

- field data collection
- stakeholder engagement
- digital system implementation
- logistics execution
- workforce onboarding
- performance monitoring

These tasks are fragmented across agencies and contractors, producing high transaction costs and delayed delivery.

---

## Policy Objective

Establish modular Civic Execution Coordination Units capable of rapidly deploying to priority national and regional projects to provide structured coordination capacity.

Units operate as temporary execution multipliers that:

- reduce project startup friction
- accelerate delivery timelines
- improve data visibility
- create institutional learning loops
- provide formative experience to service participants

---

## Unit Composition (Baseline)

A standard deployment unit consists of **24–36 personnel.**

### Leadership Layer

- 1 Execution Lead (senior practitioner; Execution Corps or certified equivalent)
- 1 Deputy Operations Lead
- 1 Data / Measurement Lead

### Technical Specialists (8–12)

Depending on domain:

- software engineers / data analysts
- healthcare systems coordinators
- industrial process technicians
- urban planning assistants
- energy systems technicians

### Logistics and Operations (8–10)

- supply chain coordinators
- scheduling and workforce planners
- procurement support staff
- deployment logistics managers

### Community Interface (4–6)

- stakeholder engagement coordinators
- communications and translation specialists
- local partnership managers

### General Service Cohort (6–10)

- early-career participants performing:
  - surveying
  - data entry and validation
  - field operations support
  - pilot implementation assistance

---

## Deployment Scale Logic

### Housing Overlay Pilot (Example)

Per district:

- 2–3 units deployed (≈60–90 personnel)

Responsibilities:

- parcel readiness verification
- permitting digitization support
- developer interface coordination
- transit integration logistics
- temporary service cohort housing activation
- real-time absorption monitoring

---

### Regional Healthcare Capacity Pilot

Per region:

- 3–5 units deployed (≈90–150 personnel)

Responsibilities:

- scheduling backlog reduction
- telehealth deployment
- patient navigation systems rollout
- insurance enrollment drives
- clinic throughput analytics
- mobile care logistics

---

### Industrial Corridor Activation

Per node:

- 4–6 units deployed (≈120–200 personnel)

Responsibilities:

- supply chain mapping
- workforce onboarding coordination
- housing and transport readiness
- equipment commissioning support
- regulatory documentation digitization

---

## Lifecycle Structure

### Phase A — Pre-Deployment Assessment (4–6 weeks)

- baseline data collection
- stakeholder mapping
- output target definition
- deployment sizing

### Phase B — Active Coordination (6–18 months)

- project execution support
- metric tracking
- adaptive resourcing

### Phase C — Transition and Wind-Down (3–6 months)

- local capability transfer
- documentation
- residual risk monitoring
- unit redeployment or dissolution

---

## Automatic Wind-Down Mechanisms

Units trigger structured reduction if:

- project output targets fall below defined thresholds for 2 consecutive quarters
- local institutional capacity reaches predefined sufficiency metrics
- coordination tasks fall below minimum utilization levels
- cost per delivered outcome exceeds benchmark bands

Wind-down includes:

- personnel reassignment
- project scope narrowing
- full dissolution if persistent underperformance is confirmed

Department of Data and Accountability maintains real-time utilization dashboards.

---

## Strategic Effects

- reduces execution bottlenecks
- enables rapid pilot scaling
- creates mobile coordination workforce
- supports regional development sequencing
- strengthens legitimacy through visible delivery
