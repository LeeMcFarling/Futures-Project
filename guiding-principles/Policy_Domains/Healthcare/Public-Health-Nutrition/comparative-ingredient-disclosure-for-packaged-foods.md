## Summary

Establish a mandatory comparative ingredient disclosure for packaged foods to expose regulatory divergence between domestic products and those sold in peer countries. The policy relies on transparency and market response rather than bans, mandates, or nutritional judgments.

## Problem Statement

A significant share of packaged foods sold domestically contain additives, processing agents, and synthetic ingredients that are restricted or prohibited in peer countries but remain legal through legacy or loophole-based pathways. Consumers lack visibility into this divergence, and manufacturers face limited pressure to justify or simplify formulations.

## Policy Objective

Create a neutral, factual market signal that:
- Makes regulatory divergence legible to consumers
- Gives manufacturers time and flexibility to respond
- Encourages reformulation or evidence-based validation
- Preserves consumer choice and supply stability

## Core Requirement

All packaged food products sold domestically must display a standardized, front-of-package disclosure if they contain ingredients that are banned or materially restricted in at least one reference jurisdiction: United Kingdom, Canada, Germany, or Australia.

## Front-of-Package Disclosure

Uniform placement and formatting across products. Neutral, factual language only.

**Standard text:**  
> Contains ingredients restricted or banned in peer countries.

No health claims, warnings, scoring systems, interpretive language, or color coding are permitted.

## Back-of-Package Ingredient Salience

Each ingredient that triggers the disclosure must appear in **bold text** within the ingredient list. Only the specific listed ingredients are bolded; the remainder of the ingredient list is unchanged.

## Public Ingredient Registry

A publicly accessible, versioned registry will list:
- Ingredient name
- Peer jurisdiction(s) where banned or restricted
- Regulatory status (ban or material restriction)
- Regulatory authority and citation
- Classification of restriction rationale (e.g., toxicology, exposure uncertainty, process-based)

The registry is auditable, updated on a fixed cadence, and maintained with full change logs.

## Manufacturer Response Pathways

Manufacturers may choose any of the following responses:
1. **Evidence submission:** Provide sufficient evidence that the ingredient is permitted under comparable food-safety standards in peer jurisdictions, triggering removal from the registry.
2. **Reformulation:** Modify recipes to eliminate listed ingredients.
3. **International harmonization:** Sell domestically the same formulations already sold in stricter markets.

No pathway is privileged or penalized.

## Governance and Review

The ingredient registry is subject to mandatory annual review. Additions and removals require published justification. No discretionary exemptions or permanent grandfathering are permitted once an ingredient is listed.

## Enforcement

Strict liability applies for non-disclosure, mislabeling, or improper formatting. Penalties scale with distribution volume. Compliance determination is binary.

## Trade and Legal Posture

This policy is a disclosure requirement, not a restriction on sale. It applies equally to domestic and imported products and relies solely on verifiable statements about foreign regulatory status, minimizing trade and constitutional risk.

## Expected Year-One Outcomes

- Increased consumer awareness of formulation divergence
- Voluntary reformulation by major manufacturers
- Gradual convergence toward simpler, internationally consistent recipes
- Establishment of a defensible transparency baseline for subsequent food and health reforms
