## Purpose and Scope

This brief defines how non-emergency healthcare services are reorganized into transparent, competitive markets. It governs **primary care, urgent care, and scheduled surgery**, and establishes clear boundaries between market-based care, utility-regulated emergency care, and insurance.

This document does **not** regulate emergency or trauma services, which are governed separately as regulated utilities. It also does not set insurance policy, except to define where insurance is prohibited from operating to prevent market distortion.

The objective is to restore price discovery, consumer choice, and competition in healthcare segments where shopping, scheduling, and informed consent are possible.

---

## Core Design Principle

Healthcare services that are predictable, schedulable, and comparable must operate as markets. Insurance is removed from these services because its presence suppresses price signals, inflates administrative overhead, and rewards opacity rather than value.

Markets are enforced, not assumed. Transparency and direct payment are mandatory conditions of participation.

---

## Primary Care — Direct-to-Consumer Market

Primary care serves as the front door to the healthcare system and operates as a direct-to-consumer market.

### Allowed Payment Models
- Subscription-based care allowed where longitudinal continuity adds value
- Per-visit pricing where episodic care is appropriate
- Primary care providers are allowed to set their own pricing and terms, with central price transparency preserved. 

### Prohibited Models
- Insurance billing or reimbursement
- Prior authorization requirements
- Mandatory membership for episodic access

### Pricing and Disclosure Requirements
- All prices must be posted, binding, and machine-readable
- Subscription offerings must disclose:
  - Scope of included services
  - Access standards and response times
  - Panel size limits
- Ancillary services (labs, imaging) must be priced transparently

### Competition Safeguards
- Anti-consolidation thresholds apply during transition
- Providers may not bundle primary care with downstream referrals or ownership-based steering

---

## Urgent Care — Episodic Market

Urgent care addresses time-sensitive but stable conditions and prevents inappropriate use of emergency departments.

### Payment Model
- Pay-per-episode only
- Subscriptions are prohibited

### Bundled Pricing
- Standardized, all-in bundles for common presentations
- Bundles include imaging, basic procedures, and follow-up instructions

### Transparency Rules
- Prices must be visible prior to treatment whenever feasible
- No post-hoc facility fees or unbundled add-ons

### Triage and Escalation Boundary
- Mandatory escalation to emergency services when defined clinical thresholds are met
- Legal safe harbor for providers who escalate appropriately
- Explicit prohibition on billing urgent care encounters as emergency services

---

## Scheduled Surgery and Complex Episodes

Planned surgeries and complex procedures operate under regulated competition and are covered by catastrophic insurance.

### Bundled Pricing Requirement
- All-in episode pricing including:
  - Facility fees
  - Professional services
  - Anesthesia
  - Devices and implants
  - Standard post-operative care

### Provider Competition
- Hospitals and ambulatory surgery centers compete on price and outcomes
- No unbundling or facility-fee gaming

### Risk Stratification and Safety
- Eligibility criteria determine appropriate site of care
- Mandatory complication insurance or reinsurance pools
- Defined transfer protocols for adverse events

---

## Competition and Anti-Gaming Rules

To preserve functioning markets:

- Facility-fee unbundling is prohibited
- Ownership-based referral steering is restricted (providers cannot refer patients to co-owned hospitals or providers)
- Price discrimination based on payer type is prohibited within market layers
- Outcome reporting is risk-adjusted and standardized

Violations trigger escalating penalties, including exclusion from regulated insurance markets.

---

## Insurance Boundary Rules

Insurance is explicitly excluded from:
- Primary care
- Urgent care
- Routine diagnostics and minor procedures

Insurance coverage applies only to:
- Scheduled surgeries
- Complex episodes
- Catastrophic events

This boundary restores actuarial integrity and prevents re-distortion of market pricing.

---

## Interface with Emergency Utility Care

Market-based care must defer to emergency utility services when clinical thresholds are met. Emergency care pricing and access obligations are governed separately.

This reform regulates market care **because emergency care is insured**, not as a substitute for insurance.

---

## Transition and Enforcement

A standing execution authority oversees:
- Market designation and certification
- Price transparency enforcement
- Anti-gaming audits
- Pilot evaluation and scaling

Rollout occurs through geographically bounded pilots with automatic safeguards to protect access and competition.

---

## Bottom Line

Care Delivery forces healthcare to behave like a system designed on purpose. Routine care becomes cheap and legible. Urgent care becomes predictable. Surgery becomes competitively priced and insurable. Insurance stops distorting daily care, and markets finally function where they are supposed to.
