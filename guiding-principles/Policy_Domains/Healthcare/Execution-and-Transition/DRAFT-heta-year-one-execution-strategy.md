# DRAFT: HETA Year One Charter and Execution Authority Framework
---
## Healthcare Execution and Transition Authority (HETA)
### Statutory Language Sketch — Integrated Deployment Model

---

### Section 1. Establishment and Purpose

There is established a Healthcare Execution and Transition Authority (HETA) as a federally
chartered execution instrument for the limited purpose of implementing, stabilizing,
and evaluating healthcare market transition pilots authorized by Congress.

HETA’s mandate is execution, coordination, and evidence generation. HETA shall not
operate care delivery, administer insurance, or develop national digital identity or
payment infrastructure.

---

### Section 2. Integrated Transition Deployment Teams

(a) **Joint Deployment Requirement**  
For each approved pilot state or cohort, HETA shall deploy **Integrated Transition
Teams** composed of:

- HETA execution personnel  
- Public Capital Authority (PCA) personnel, where capital deployment or financing
  instruments are implicated  
- Designated state regulatory representatives appointed by the participating state  
- Representatives of the Severe Event Coverage (SEC) administering body, where
  emergency, catastrophic, or bundled episode coverage is implicated  

No federal transition activity shall occur in isolation from state participation.

(b) **Purpose of Co-Deployment**  
Integrated Transition Teams exist to:
- Resolve operational and regulatory conflicts in real time  
- Align federal execution, state regulation, and capital deployment  
- Prevent jurisdictional delay, blame-shifting, or regulatory deadlock  
- Ensure that transition decisions reflect on-the-ground conditions  

(c) **Non-Preemption Clause**  
Co-deployment does not constitute federal preemption of state authority except where
explicitly authorized under Market Transition Waivers. State representatives retain
their full statutory authority.

---

### Section 3. Market Transition Waiver Authority

(a) **Authorization**  
HETA is granted authority to issue **Market Transition Waivers** solely to enable
approved healthcare transition pilots to function during the transition from the
legacy regulatory framework to the restructured healthcare market.

(b) **Joint Issuance and Review**  
Where a waiver implicates state regulatory authority, the waiver shall be:
- Issued jointly with the designated state regulatory representative, or
- Issued by HETA following documented consultation if joint issuance is not feasible

All waivers shall be explicitly labeled and published as *Market Transition Waivers*.

(c) **Limitations**  
Market Transition Waivers:
- Must be tied to a named statute or regulation
- Must specify the operational barrier addressed
- Must be pilot-scoped, purpose-bound, and time-limited
- Shall automatically expire unless affirmatively renewed

This authority shall not extend to digital identity systems, payment rails, or financial
infrastructure regulation.

---

### Section 4. Waiver Revocation and Enforcement

HETA retains authority to suspend or revoke any Market Transition Waiver upon evidence
of abuse, gaming, fraud, anti-competitive conduct, or threats to access or safety.

Revocation decisions shall be made in coordination with state regulatory representatives
where applicable and reported to Congress.

---

### Section 5. Performance-Gated Expansion

(a) **Unified Performance Gates**  
Expansion of pilot participation, transition phase escalation, or geographic scope
shall be contingent upon performance gates jointly evaluated by:
- HETA
- Participating state regulators
- PCA (where capital instruments are involved)
- SEC administering body (where applicable)

(b) **Gate Criteria** include:
- Clinician participation and retention
- Patient access continuity
- Transparency and compliance integrity
- Administrative burden reduction
- Absence of material gaming or market foreclosure

Failure to clear gates triggers pause, rule adjustment, or rollback rather than expansion.

---

### Section 6. Congressional Oversight and Learning

HETA shall submit an annual **Transition Findings Report** to Congress describing:
- What worked and what failed operationally
- Which regulations blocked transition and which proved unnecessary
- How federal, state, and capital authorities interacted in practice
- Recommendations for the steady-state regulatory framework

Congress retains authority to modify, expand, or sunset HETA but shall not be required
to approve individual execution actions taken within the authorized charter.

---

### Section 7. Sunset

All extraordinary authorities granted under this Act, including Market Transition
Waiver authority, shall sunset unless affirmatively reauthorized by Congress following
review of the Transition Findings Report.

---

### Section 8. Construction

Nothing in this Act shall be construed to:
- Establish a national healthcare payer
- Nationalize healthcare delivery
- Deregulate healthcare markets broadly
- Supersede state authority except as explicitly provided

This Act establishes a **co-deployed execution and learning instrument**, not a permanent
regulatory regime.
