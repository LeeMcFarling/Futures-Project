## Purpose

This brief defines a pilot network of regional clinical trial infrastructure designed to reduce execution latency, operational risk, and measurement inconsistency in pharmaceutical, nutritional, and preventive research. These facilities exist to accelerate trials, not to replace hospitals, CROs, or private research networks.

They are shared, optional execution infrastructure that any qualified sponsor may use on a neutral, fee-for-service basis.

## Problem Statement

Clinical trials are delayed and made expensive by overloaded sites, slow activation, inconsistent execution quality, and fragmented measurement standards. Early-phase trials are particularly constrained by scarce specialized facilities, while nutrition and prevention studies suffer from poor retention and inconsistent data collection.

Sponsors compensate by overbuilding protocols, over-monitoring sites, and avoiding low-margin indications altogether. This is an execution failure, not a scientific one.

## Scope and Non-Goals

These facilities:
- Do not provide general medical care
- Do not replace sponsor-selected sites
- Do not mandate use by any trial
- Do not own patient relationships or data
- Do not set prices or trial designs

They exist solely to provide high-reliability clinical trial execution capacity.

## Facility Model

### Regional Hubs

The pilot consists of a limited number of regional hubs aligned to population density and research logistics. Each hub is designed for high-throughput, protocol-compliant execution rather than bespoke care delivery.

Functions include:
- Rapid site activation
- Early-phase (Phase I / IIa) trial execution
- Standardized labs and measurement protocols
- Hybrid and decentralized trial support

### Satellite and Hybrid Support

Regional hubs may coordinate with community hospitals, clinics, and mobile staff to support decentralized trials. The hub acts as the compliance and data anchor, enabling participation by sites that would otherwise be excluded.

## Eligible Uses

Facilities may be used for:
- Pharmaceutical and biologic trials
- Nutrition and metabolic studies
- Preventive and public health research
- Methodology pilots and measurement validation

Public-need trials may receive priority access or discounted rates during the pilot period.

## Integration with Outreach Reform

These facilities integrate directly with the Clinical Trial Outreach and Recruitment framework. They serve as execution endpoints for participants identified through patient-controlled eligibility discovery.

Recruitment remains patient-pull and opt-in. Facilities do not conduct outreach themselves except for operational coordination after consent.

## Integration with Manufacturing

Regional clinical hubs interface with modular therapeutic manufacturing facilities for clinical supply. This enables rapid transition from lab to first-in-human studies without sponsor-specific plant buildout.

## Data and Measurement Standards

Facilities operate on shared, published standards:
- Standardized protocol templates
- Validated lab and measurement procedures
- Interoperable data formats
- Audit-ready monitoring logs

Facilities do not claim ownership of sponsor data or IP.

## Access and Governance

Access is open to any qualified sponsor. Pricing, availability, and eligibility criteria are published in advance.

Governance principles:
- Neutral access
- No IP claims
- Firewalled sponsor data
- Independent audits
- Performance-based review

## Metrics

Pilot success is evaluated on:
- Time-to-first-patient
- Site activation time
- Cost per enrolled participant
- Retention and completion rates
- Protocol deviation frequency

## Sunset and Scale

The pilot includes statutory sunset provisions. Continuation or expansion requires demonstrated cost reduction, utilization, and sponsor adoption.

Facilities that fail to meet benchmarks are wound down or repurposed.

## Relationship to Broader Pharma R&D Reform

This infrastructure, combined with outreach reform and modular manufacturing, forms a bounded R&D de-risking stack. It lowers fixed costs and execution risk without altering safety standards, IP ownership, or market pricing dynamics.
