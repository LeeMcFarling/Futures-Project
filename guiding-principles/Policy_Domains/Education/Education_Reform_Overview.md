#### **Summary**

This policy proposes transitioning U.S. K–12 education toward a software-defined, AI-enabled model that standardizes core curriculum by methodology, enables hyper-personalized learning paths, and repositions teachers as facilitators of holistic development. The system is built on open-source infrastructure, strong privacy guarantees (SSI + zero-knowledge proofs), and mastery-based progression rather than seat-time. The objective is to raise baseline outcomes, reduce geographic inequality, improve resilience and flexibility, and enable future interoperability with allied education systems without cultural or political coercion.

#### **Problem Statement**

The current education system is uneven, brittle, and time-bound. Outcomes depend heavily on ZIP code, rigid schedules designed for industrial-era labor patterns, and teacher availability. Curriculum updates are slow, personalization is limited, teacher burnout is high, and parental trust is eroding. At the same time, incompatible standards prevent meaningful domestic portability and international cooperation in education.


#### **Core Proposal**

1. Software-Defined Core Curriculum

Establish a nationally interoperable core curriculum standardized by methodology and learning objectives, not fixed content sequences. AI systems manage diagnostics, pacing, feedback, and mastery tracking. Curriculum units are versioned, forkable, and auditable.

2. Teachers as Facilitators

Teachers shift from primary lecturers to learning facilitators, mentors, and project leads. Their focus moves toward social development, interdisciplinary projects, applied learning, and student well-being. Career ladders expand to include facilitator, mentor, curriculum contributor, and project architect roles.

3. Mastery-Based Progression

Students advance based on demonstrated understanding rather than time spent in class. Learning state is persistent and portable, allowing acceleration, pauses, or relocation without academic penalty.

4. Flexible Scheduling

Where appropriate, core academic instruction compresses into approximately two hours per day. Remaining time is allocated to projects, physical activity, life skills, arts, and social development. Schools pilot varied schedule models aligned with family and community needs.


#### **Privacy, Identity, and Governance**

- Student learning data is de-identified by default; PII is stored separately with strict access controls.
- Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) governs authorization and access.
- Zero-knowledge proofs enable parents or guardians to verify access rights (e.g., “guardian-of”) without exposing excess personal data.
- Parents can view what their child learned and the associated lesson materials, but cannot override instruction in real time.
- All data access is logged, auditable, and subject to independent review.

#### **Open-Source Model**

- The core education software stack is open source and publicly owned.
- Baseline funding supports maintenance and security.
- Periodic, scoped contribution rounds reward software engineers and teams for measurable improvements in performance, fairness, accessibility, or efficiency.
- Winning contributions are compensated, credited, and merged through a strict maintainer and review process.

#### **Pilot Strategy**

Phase 1: Domestic Core Pilots
    - Opt-in districts across diverse regions
    - Limited subject domains (e.g., math, literacy, science fundamentals)
    - Clear metrics: mastery, engagement, attendance, teacher retention, parental trust

Phase 2: Expanded Domestic Experiments
    - Flexible scheduling models
    - Teacher rotation and sabbatical coverage
    - Transparent comparison of school-level pilots

Phase 3: International Interoperability Pilots
    - Bilateral or small-bloc pilots with allied countries
    - Shared infrastructure with sovereign curriculum forks
    - Study-abroad and exchange programs without credit loss


#### **Risks and Mitigations**
- Over-optimization on test scores → prioritize long-term mastery and transfer
- Surveillance concerns → strict data minimization and no behavioral scoring
- Equity gaps → universal access guarantees, no premium tiers
- Teacher resistance → parallel upskilling, workload reduction, and role elevation

Strategic Impact
- Raises the national educational floor while preserving local autonomy
- Improves teacher retention and reduces burnout
- Makes education portable, resilient, and compatible with modern family life
- Enables cooperative, non-imperial educational interoperability with allies
- Repositions the U.S. as a visible learner and co-builder of systems

#### **Conclusion**

This proposal treats education as critical civic infrastructure rather than a static institution. By combining AI-driven personalization, open governance, and strong privacy guarantees, it enables continuous improvement without sacrificing sovereignty, trust, or human development. Over time, it strengthens domestic outcomes while supporting a cooperative international order grounded in shared standards and mutual respect.
