## Purpose

This brief proposes a structural reorientation of university research toward discovery pre-seeding. Its objective is to realign academic incentives with genuine scientific discovery, reduce incumbency bias, and create durable funding pathways for universities and researchers as the four-year degree model declines in centrality.

Universities are positioned not as venture studios or credential monopolies, but as **option generators**: institutions optimized to explore uncertainty, validate foundational results, and surface discoveries worthy of downstream scaling.

## Problem Statement

The current university research model is strained by conflicting roles:

- Mass credentialing tied to four-year degrees
- Prestige-driven signaling and incumbency bias
- Grant structures favoring incremental, low-risk work
- Weak and distorted pathways from discovery to application

As a result, reputational advantage often outweighs discovery quality, early-stage ideas are oversold into premature startups, and genuine scientific exploration is under-rewarded.

## Core Principle

**Universities should generate discovery options; society should decide which ones to scale.**

Academic research excels at exploration and validation under uncertainty. Translation, capital deployment, and operational scaling are better handled by downstream actors with aligned incentives and risk-bearing capacity.

## Discovery Units

University research outputs are formalized as **Discovery Units** rather than startups.

A Discovery Unit includes:
- Validated experimental or theoretical results
- Methods, protocols, and datasets
- Replication and reproducibility metadata
- Documented negative results where relevant
- Clearly scoped IP claims or open-licensing status

Discovery Units are discrete, reviewable research assets designed for selection, not promotion.

## Funding Stack

### Baseline Public Funding

Public funding supports:
- High-uncertainty exploration
- Replication and foundational validation
- Shared research infrastructure

This layer absorbs risk that markets cannot efficiently price.

### Discovery Unit Buyouts

Downstream actors (pharma, materials firms, public authorities) may:
- License Discovery Units
- Purchase them outright
- Enter co-development agreements

Buyouts generate non-dilutive revenue for universities and direct compensation for researchers.

### Portfolio Sustainability

Most Discovery Units will not be commercialized. A minority of high-value selections cross-subsidize exploration, reducing reliance on grant cycles and prestige-based funding.

## Researcher Incentives

Researchers benefit through:
- Revenue-sharing from Discovery Unit selection
- Reputation tied to reuse and validation of work
- Career mobility across academia, industry, and public research

This shifts incentives toward originality, rigor, and replication rather than volume or signaling.

## Guardrails

To prevent exploitation and capture:

- Minimum buyout pricing floors
- Mandatory revenue-sharing with originating institutions and researchers
- Right of publication protections
- Anti-exclusivity and anti-hoarding rules
- Public-interest licensing fallback for critical domains

## Relationship to Education Reform

As degree pathways modularize, universities increasingly differentiate through:
- Research apprenticeships
- Contribution-based credentials
- Discovery participation rather than seat time

Discovery pre-seeding integrates naturally with modular education and lifelong learning models.

## Relationship to the Futures Project

This framework supplies the upstream discovery layer for the broader execution architecture:

- Discovery Units feed de-risked R&D pipelines
- Certification clarity and stage-based authorization govern downstream progression
- Recoupment-bound pricing ensures fair value distribution

Universities become the frontier of uncertainty exploration rather than the bottleneck of translation.

## Expected Impact

- Reduced incumbency bias in research funding
- Increased tolerance for exploratory and negative results
- New, durable revenue streams for universities
- Faster and more honest translation of discovery into public value
