Policy Brief: Book Recommendation & Supervised Social Learning Pilot

Purpose
Student literacy and reading engagement have declined sharply, with wide variance in reading ability, interest, and emotional maturity within the same classrooms. Static reading lists and one-size-fits-all assignments fail to meet students where they are, leading to disengagement, avoidance, and stalled skill development.

This pilot establishes a personalized book recommendation and supervised social learning system that enables students to discover reading material aligned with their interests, age, emotional maturity, and reading level, grounded in a standardized, transparent rating framework. The objective is to restore reading as a habit by making discovery relevant, accessible, and appropriately challenging.

The system also matches students reading the same material at the same time into small, supervised cohorts. These cohorts support structured discussion of themes, ideas, and content, reinforcing comprehension, empathy, and critical thinking while modeling healthy, pro-social digital interaction. Literacy gains are driven not only by better material matching, but by shared meaning-making under guidance.

Problem Addressed
Students already spend significant time in online social spaces optimized for engagement extraction rather than development. Schools largely abstain from digital socialization, leaving students to internalize norms from unregulated platforms. Meanwhile, literacy declines and meaningful discussion is often avoided due to controversy risk. The result is weaker reading outcomes, poor discourse norms, and higher exposure to harmful online dynamics.

Core Design Principle
The system is opt-in, supervised, and purpose-built. It does not replicate commercial social media. Discovery is guided rather than viral; interaction is contextual and bounded; expression is encouraged within clear norms. Friction is intentionally introduced where it improves quality and safety.

Recommendation Engine
Books are recommended using transparent, non-extractive criteria:
	•	Reading difficulty progression
	•	Age and emotional maturity alignment
	•	Student interests (opt-in, coarse-grained)
	•	Recently completed or concurrent readings

All recommendations draw exclusively from the rated and tagged corpus defined by the Standardized Book Rating System. There is no infinite scroll, no follower counts, and no engagement optimization.

Supervised Social Learning Layer
Students participate in small, time-bound discussion cohorts tied to specific books or themes. Interaction is asynchronous by default to reduce performative pressure and encourage reflection. Prompts emphasize comprehension, interpretation, perspective-taking, and respectful disagreement. Visibility is limited to defined peer groups (class, grade band, or school).

Scheduled Live Discussion Sessions
For deeper engagement, the system supports scheduled live discussion sessions that must be created in advance, mirroring adult academic and workplace meeting norms.

Live sessions:
	•	Are opt-in and tied to specific reading milestones or themes
	•	Occur in small groups with defined start and end times
	•	Are visible to assigned moderators, who may observe or intervene

This structure teaches students how to propose discussions, coordinate schedules, prepare for focused conversation, respect shared time, and engage productively in real-time dialogue. Live interaction is treated as valuable and intentional, not spontaneous or chaotic.

Sessions are not open chat rooms. There is no mass joining, no viral escalation, and no unstructured real-time feeds. Monitoring prioritizes safety and guidance, not surveillance. No permanent recording or behavioral scoring is permitted by default, and access is strictly limited.

Role of Educators & Moderators
Teachers and designated moderators:
	•	Establish discussion norms
	•	Curate prompts and pacing
	•	Guide conversation when needed
	•	Model constructive discourse

Intervention emphasizes instruction and redirection over punishment. There is no public ranking, popularity signaling, or algorithmic amplification of conflict.

Student Agency & Protections
Participation levels are adjustable. Students may read without posting, post without attending live sessions, or disengage temporarily. Identity is persistent within the school context but not public-facing. No engagement streaks, reaction counts, or behavioral scores are allowed.

Parental Transparency
Parents can view:
	•	Recommended books and reading paths
	•	Content tags and difficulty bands
	•	The structure and rules governing discussions

They cannot observe peer conversations in real time, preserving developmental space, but may raise concerns through defined review channels.

Technology & Governance Constraints
The platform is open source, publicly governed, and auditable. Algorithms are explainable at the system level. No advertising, data resale, or cross-platform tracking is permitted. Logs exist solely for safety, fairness, and dispute resolution.

Pilot Scope
The pilot operates in opt-in schools, initially focusing on:
	•	Middle school and early high school cohorts
	•	A limited but diverse book catalog
	•	Clearly scoped discussion formats and timelines

Success Metrics
	•	Reading engagement and completion rates
	•	Literacy growth relative to baseline
	•	Quality of discussion (rubric-based, not volume-based)
	•	Student-reported digital wellbeing
	•	Parent and teacher trust scores

Risks & Mitigations
	•	Replication of unhealthy social dynamics → mitigated by small groups, scheduling friction, and no popularity signals
	•	Ideological disputes → mitigated by transparent tagging and facilitator norms
	•	Scope creep → mitigated by strict educational purpose and no external integrations

Strategic Impact
	•	Improves literacy through relevance and peer reinforcement
	•	Replaces unmanaged social media exposure with a structured alternative
	•	Teaches coordination, discourse, and digital citizenship by practice
	•	Reduces cultural conflict through clarity and process
	•	Integrates cleanly with AI personalization without surveillance

Conclusion
This pilot treats literacy, social learning, and digital norms as inseparable. By pairing personalized discovery with supervised, intentionally structured interaction—including scheduled live discussions—it strengthens reading skills, models adult civic behavior, and restores trust without outsourcing development to extractive platforms.
