## The decision you’re actually making

This isn’t just sequencing (“write now vs later”). It’s about **what level of abstraction this doc should live at right now**.

There are two viable paths:

### Option A — Fully specify now (high risk)

You try to fully define:

* University ↔ manufacturing corridors
* University ↔ labor flows
* University ↔ Big Ask portfolio

Problem: you’ll hardcode assumptions that will likely be wrong once those domains are built out. You’ll create inconsistencies that require rewrite.

### Option B — Define the invariant + leave interfaces open (correct move)

You lock:

* What universities **are**
* What they **produce**
* What they are **not responsible for anymore**

And you explicitly defer:

* Where outputs go
* How labor flows through
* How national problems are structured

This keeps the document **stable while everything around it evolves**.

## Interfaces, Dependencies, and Open Questions (Recommended Addition)

This framework intentionally defines the **internal logic of the university layer** while leaving its downstream interfaces partially unspecified.

The full operational role of universities depends on three external systems currently under development:

**1. Industrial and Manufacturing Systems**
How Discovery Units are selected, translated, and scaled within regional and national production systems is defined in the manufacturing and trade domains. Universities generate validated options; they do not determine which options are scaled or how.

**2. Labor and Reskilling Systems**
The flow of individuals into and out of universities at different life stages — early talent, mid-career reskilling, domain specialization — is governed by the labor and credentialing architecture. Universities provide environments for capability development and contribution, but do not act as primary credential gatekeepers.

**3. National Problem Portfolio (“Big Asks”)**
The directional allocation of research attention — which problems are prioritized, funded, and evaluated at the national or state level — is defined outside the university system. Universities respond to these problem portfolios by generating Discovery Units relevant to defined domains, but do not control the prioritization mechanism.

**Design Principle:**
Universities are designed as **option generators under uncertainty**.
They are intentionally decoupled from:

* final selection decisions
* capital allocation decisions
* labor market certification authority

This decoupling prevents incentive distortion and preserves the exploratory function that no other institution performs well.
