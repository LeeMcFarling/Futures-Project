## Core Axiom

Sustained cooperation within and between societies depends on credible belief in survivable and meaningful futures.

Governance systems that fail to maintain this belief increase the probability of political instability, economic volatility, conflict escalation, and institutional breakdown.

The Futures Project treats preservation of existential stability as a foundational design objective across domestic policy, economic architecture, security frameworks, and international governance.

---

## Definition — Mortality Salience (Systemic Governance Context)

Mortality salience refers to a heightened perception that material or symbolic systems necessary for long-term survival and meaningful societal participation are unreliable, failing, or under threat.

This perception may arise from:

- direct exposure to violence, regime instability, infrastructure vulnerability, or economic collapse  
- sustained polarization, identity threat framing, or erosion of institutional legitimacy  
- breakdown of symbolic meaning systems such as belief in social mobility, civic fairness, national continuity, or cooperative international order  

Elevated mortality salience tends to:

- harden identity boundaries  
- shorten political and economic time horizons  
- increase support for authoritarian or absolutist leadership  
- reduce empathy across social, ideological, or national groups  
- accelerate capital volatility and risk-avoidant behavior  
- weaken democratic deliberation and cooperative security arrangements  

Reducing structural drivers of mortality salience is therefore treated as a core objective of resilient governance design.

---

## Material and Symbolic Protection Systems

Existential stability depends on perceived reliability of both material and symbolic protection systems.

### Material protection systems

- physical security and deterrence credibility  
- economic stability and access to meaningful work  
- housing availability and community continuity  
- infrastructure reliability  
- predictable alliance commitments  
- rule of law and crisis-response competence  

### Symbolic protection systems

- belief in fair opportunity and upward mobility  
- civic identity and belonging  
- institutional legitimacy  
- cultural or religious meaning frameworks  
- future-oriented national narratives  
- dignity pathways linked to contribution  

Failure in either domain can produce similar destabilizing effects.

## Intra-Country Causality Chain

Structural shocks → elevated mortality salience → political hardening → institutional degradation.

Illustrative pathway:

economic stagnation or housing scarcity  
→ symbolic contract erosion (belief in meritocratic advancement weakens)  
→ mortality salience rises  
→ polarization and grievance politics intensify  
→ demand for strong central authority increases  
→ democratic norms and institutional trust weaken  
→ governance volatility rises  
→ investment confidence and long-term growth decline  

---

## Inter-Country Causality Chain

Regional instability → elevated mortality salience → alliance fragility → escalation risk.

Illustrative pathway:

persistent gray-zone attacks or conflict exposure  
→ perception that protection systems may fail  
→ capital flight and fiscal stress  
→ political decision horizons shorten  
→ hedging behavior and unilateral security moves increase  
→ cooperative security frameworks weaken  
→ adversary coercive leverage grows  
→ probability of escalation or fragmentation rises  

---

## Design Implication

Modern governance architectures must be evaluated not only on efficiency, growth, or deterrence outcomes, but on their ability to reduce background existential threat perception.

Systems that:

- extend decision time horizons  
- stabilize access to meaning and dignity pathways  
- increase predictability of security and economic outcomes  
- distribute risk across cooperative networks  

are structurally more likely to sustain democratic legitimacy, alliance durability, and long-term peace.

Existential stability is therefore treated as a cross-domain performance variable rather than a purely psychological or sociological concern.

---

## Relationship to Futures Project Instruments

Key initiatives intended to reduce systemic mortality salience include:

- Integrated Defense Partnerships (distributed deterrence credibility)  
- counter-cyclical economic stabilization mechanisms  
- housing and infrastructure abundance frameworks  
- capital allocation transparency and fairness reforms  
- layered regional and global legitimacy architectures  

These mechanisms collectively increase belief in navigable futures at both individual and state levels.
